Grand Funk Railroad Denied Injunction To Block Mark Farner From Using The American Band

 Posted on September 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  447 words  |  Larita Shotwell

DETROIT (CelebrityAccess) — A federal court in Michigan has denied Grand Funk Railroad’s motion for preliminary injunction to block the band’s former frontman Mark Farner from using the name “Mark Farner’s American Band.”

On Thursday, Honorable Gordon J. Quist, United States District Court Judge for the Western District of Michigan, ruled that GFR had failed to demonstrate how Farner’s use of the name ‘Mark Farner’s American Band’ creates the likelihood of confusion among consumers.

GFR had argued a case for infringement, noting that “The American Band” is a ‘famous mark’ that has been in use by GFR, including its albums and promotional material for 45 years.

However, the judge ruled against GFR and in his opinion, noted: “Initially, while the Court has no doubt that GFR’s GRAND FUNK marks are famous, the Court is not prepared at this juncture—without an evidentiary basis—to conclude that THE AMERICAN BAND is a famous mark.”

The ruling on the preliminary injunction motion does not end the case; it merely allows Farner to use the disputed name while the case proceeds to discovery and beyond.

Just in time for Farner, who is planning to tour with his newly-affirmed band name. The tour kicks off later this week with a pair of shows at the Boot Barn in Colorado Springs and wraps on July 19th at Rocking The River in Detroit, Mich.
