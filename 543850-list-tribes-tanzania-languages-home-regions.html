List of all tribes in Tanzania with their languages and home regions

49.zania is home to many fascinating tribes that have significantly shaped the country's rich tapestry of traditions and customs. From the iconic Maasai warriors with their distinctive red clothing and intricate beadwork to the Hadzabe hunter-gatherers living harmoniously with nature, tribes in Tanzania offer a unique glimpse into the vibrancy and diversity of this East African nation.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

Tanzania has over 120 ethnic groups, categorised into Bantu, Nilotic, and non-African minorities. These tribes contribute to the country's rich cultural diversity, with each tribe having its own language, traditions, and customs.

Agriculture and livestock herding are essential to the livelihoods of many tribes in Tanzania. The Chagga tribe, for example, is known for its strong work ethic and farming practices. Although all tribes have their own languages and unique ways of life, they are all unified by Swahili, the national language.

What are the tribes in Tanzania?

Different tribes have existed in Tanzania for centuries. They have preserved their customs and heritage while also adapting to modern times. Understanding the diversity of Tanzania's tribes is crucial for appreciating the country's vibrant culture and history. Here is a list of all tribes in Tanzania and their origins.

1. Akie

The Akie live in the western Arusha Region. They are sometimes derogatorily called Dorobo or Wandorobo and speak the Akie language.

2. Alagwa

The Alagwa are a Cushitic ethnic group primarily based in central Tanzania's Kondoa District of the Dodoma Region. Some can also be found in the Hanang district of the Manyara Region. They speak the Alagwa language.

3. Arusha

The Arusha live on the western slopes of Mount Meru in the Arusha District of the Arusha Region. They speak the Maasai language.

4. Assa

The Assa are a Cushitic ethnic group based on the Maasai Steppe in the Manyara Region of Tanzania. They once spoke the Aasáx language, which is now extinct.

5. Barabaig

The Barabaig are a pastoralist group living on the high plateau surrounding Mount Hanang. They speak a dialect of the Datooga language, a Nilotic language spoken by the Datooga people of the Great Rift Valley of Tanzania.

6. Bembe

The Bembe are an ethnic and linguistic group based in western Tanzania. They represent various ethnic traditions, including pre-Lega, Boyo-Kunda, Hemba, and Bemba.

They are primarily located in the Kigoma region, occupying the entire coastal region of Lake Tanganyika, from Kagunga to Mgambo, and speak the Bembe language.

7. Bena

The Bena are a Bantu group residing in southwestern Tanzania, particularly in the Iringa Region, Njombe District. They speak the Bena language.

8. Bende

The Bende are a Bantu ethnic group primarily based in the Mpanda District of the Katavi Region in western Tanzania. They speak the Bende language. Their home regions within Tanzania include the Kabungu, Karema, and Mwese divisions.

9. Bondei

The Bondei are a Bantu ethnic group residing in the Pangani District in the east Tanga Region of northeastern Tanzania. They live in the Usambara Mountains, inland from Tanga.

The Bondei speak a Bantu language called Bondei or Bonde, which neighbouring languages like Doe and Kwere have influenced.

10. Bungu

The Bungu, also known as the Wungu, are a Bantu ethnolinguistic group native to the Chunya District of Mbeya Region in southwestern Tanzania. They speak the Bungu language, which is classified as Niger-Congo.

11. Burunge

The Burunge people are a small community in Tanzania. They reside primarily in the Dodoma Region, specifically in the Northeastern region. They are part of the Southern Cushites, a cluster of Tanzanian groups.

The Burunge speak the Burunge language and belong to the South Cushitic language family within the larger Afro-Asiatic family.

12. Chagga

The Chagga are a Bantu ethnic group in the Kilimanjaro Region. They speak Kichagga, which is spoken in various dialects in different regions. The Chagga are considered the most beautiful tribe in Tanzania.

13. Datooga

The Datooga, also known as the Mang'ati in Swahili, are an ethnic group from the Karatu District of the Arusha Region. They historically live in the southwest Manyara Region and northern Singida Region of Tanzania.

14. Dhaiso

The Dhaiso, also known as the Wadaiso or Daiso in Swahili, is a Bantu ethnolinguistic group based in the Muheza District of the Tanga Region in northeastern Tanzania. They speak the Dhaiso language, also known as Kidhaiso.

15. Digo

The Digo are a Bantu ethnic and linguistic group based near the Indian Ocean coast between Mombasa in southern Kenya and Tanga in northern Tanzania.

They are part of the Mijikenda ethnic group, which consists of nine smaller groups or tribes. The Digo people are nearly all Muslims and speak the Digo language, called Chidigo by speakers, a Bantu language.

16. Doe

The Doe are an ethnic and linguistic group based in northern coastal Tanzania. They primarily live in the Bagamoyo District and Chalinze District of Pwani Region. Their language, the Doe (Dohe), is a Bantu language spoken in the Pwani Region.

17. Fipa

The Fipa, or Wafipa, are a Bantu ethno-linguistic group based in the Sumbawanga Rural and Nkasi districts of the Rukwa Region in southwestern Tanzania. They speak the Fipa language, which is a Bantu.

18. Gogo

The Gogo are a Bantu ethnic and linguistic group based in the Dodoma Region of central Tanzania. They speak the Cigogo language and belong to the eastern Bantu language group.

19. Goma

The Goma are a Bantu tribe in western Tanzania's Kigoma Region. They are also known as Al Ghamawiyyun in Arabic and are believed to have originated from Sudan. The Goma people speak the Goma language, a dialect of the Bantu language.

20. Gorowa

The Gorowa people are a Cushitic ethnic group and Iraqw community inhabiting Tanzania's Manyara and Dodoma regions. They speak the Gorowa language as a mother tongue, which belongs to the South Cushitic branch of the Afro-Asiatic family. The Gorowa language is also known as Fiome, Goroa, Gorwaa, Kimbulu, and Ufiomi.

21. Ha

The Ha, also known as Abaha or Waha in Swahili, are a Bantu ethnic group bordering Lake Tanganyika in the Kigoma Region of northwestern Tanzania.

They speak the Ha language, also called Kiha, Ikiha, or Giha. This language is closely related to the Kirundi and Kinyarwanda spoken in neighbouring Burundi and Rwanda and belongs to the Niger-Congo family of languages.

22. Hadimu

The Hadimu, also known as Wahadimu in Swahili, are a Bantu ethnic and linguistic group native to Zanzibar and Pemba Island in Tanzania. Until recently, they spoke their unique language, Kihadimu, but now most speak Swahili, albeit with an odd accent.

23. Hadza

The Hadza live near Lake Eyasi in northern Tanzania, specifically near the Karatu district or Arusha region. They speak the Hadza language, known as Hadzane, a click language, and Swahili as a second language.

24. Hangaza

The Hangaza are a Bantu ethnolinguistic group based in the Ngara District of Kagera Region in northwestern Tanzania. They speak the Hangaza language, which is closely related to and partially intelligible with the languages of Rwanda and Burundi.

25. Haya

The Haya are a Bantu ethnic group based in the Kagera Region in Bukoba, Muleba, and Karagwe, northwestern Tanzania, on the western side of Lake Victoria. They speak the Haya language, also known as OruHaya.

26. Hehe

The Hehe are a Bantu-speaking agricultural group in southern Tanzania's Iringa region. They speak the Hehe language, known as Kihehe in Swahili, which is closely related to the neighbouring KiBena language.

27. Holoholo

The Holoholo, also known as Kalanga or Wakalanga in Swahili, are a Bantu ethnic group inhabiting central Lake Tanganyika's shores. Most of them live near Kalemie city on Lake Tanganyika and on the opposite shore of the lake in Uvinza. They speak the Holoholo language, a Bantu language.

28. Ikizu

The Ikizu are an ethnic group found in the Tanzanian region of Mwanza, particularly in the districts of Magu and Geita. They speak the Ikizu language, a Bantu language.

29. Ikoma

The Ikoma are an ethnic and linguistic group based in the Mara Region in northern Tanzania. They speak the Ikoma language, which is easily understood by other Bantu people living in East Africa.

30. Iramba

The Iramba primarily reside in the Kishapu District of the Shinyanga Region and the Iramba District of the Singida Region in central Tanzania. They are a Bantu-speaking ethnic group, and their particular Bantu language is known as Kiniramba.

31. Iraqw

The Iraqw are an ethnic group residing in northern Tanzania, particularly in the Arusha and Manyara regions near the Rift Valley. They are predominantly found in the southeast of Ngorongoro Crater in the Karatu District and the Mbulu Highlands in the Manyara Region.

They speak the Iraqw language, which belongs to the South Cushitic branch of the Afro-Asiatic family.

32. Isanzu

The Isanzu are found in northern Tanzania, specifically in the Singida region, Iramba district. They speak the Isanzu language, a Bantu language.

33. Jiji

The Jiji, also known as Bajiji (Wajiji in Swahili), are a Bantu ethnic and linguistic group based in Kigoma Region, Tanzania. Bajiji (Jiji people) are part of Baha (Ha people) since their language is the same.

34. Jita

The Jita are primarily located around Mount Masita on the eastern side of Lake Victoria in Tanzania. They are based in the Mara Region in northern Tanzania, specifically in Rorya District and speak the Jita language.

35. Kaguru

The Kaguru are a Bantu ethnolinguistic group based in the Gairo District and Kilosa District of Morogoro Region of Tanzania. They speak the Kagulu language, a Bantu language closely related to Gogo and Zaramo.

36. Kami

The Kami live in the Morogoro Region, Tanzania. They speak the Kami language, a Bantu language that shares substantial lexical similarities with Kutu, Kwere, Zaramo, Doe, and Luguru.

37. Kara

The Kara is based in the Ukerewe District of the Mwanza Region, in the Tanzanian section of Lake Victoria (Ukara Island, Ukerewe Island, southeastern lake shore). They speak the Kara language.

38. Kerewe

The Kerewe or Wakerewe are a Bantu ethnolinguistic group based on Ukerewe Island in the Tanzanian section of Lake Victoria. They speak the Kerewe language.

39. Kimbu

The Kimbu live in the Chunya District of Mbeya Region, Tanzania. They speak the Kimbu language.

40. Kinga

The Kinga are an ethnic and linguistic group native to the Mbeya Region and Makete District of the Njombe Region. Among the many tribes in Tanzania and their regions, the Kimbu occupy the great Kipengere Range northeast of Lake Malawi. They speak the Kinga language.

41. Kisi

The Kisi are a Bantu ethnolinguistic group from the Makete District of Njombe Region, Tanzania, on the northwestern shore of Lake Malawi. They speak the Kisi language.

42. Konongo

The Konongo people of Tanzania are a Bantu group primarily based in the Mpanda District of the Katavi Region in western Tanzania. They speak the Konongo language.

43. Kuria

The Kuria (also known as the AbaKurya, are a Bantu community in the Tarime District of the Mara Region in Tanzania and southern Kenya. Their homeland is bounded east by the Migori River and west by the Mara River estuary. They speak the Kuria language.

44. Kutu

The Kutu are a matrilineal ethnic and linguistic group based in the Morogoro Region of central Tanzania. They speak the Kutu language.

45. Kw'adza

The Kw'adza, also known as the Qwadza, are an ethnic group based in the Mbulu District of Manyara Region, Tanzania. Their mother tongue is the Kw'adza language, which belongs to the South Cushitic branch of the Afro-Asiatic family.

46. Kwaya

The Kwaya are a Bantu ethnolinguistic group based in the Mara Region of northern Tanzania, on the southeastern shore of Lake Victoria. They speak the Kwaya language.

47. Kwere

The Kwere, also known as Ngh'wele (Wakwere in Swahili), are a matrilineal ethnic and linguistic group native to Bagamoyo District and Chalinze District in the Pwani Region of coastal Tanzania. They speak the Ngh'wele language, which is called Kikwere in Swahili.

48. Lambya

The Lambya are a minority based along the border of northwestern Malawi, Ileje, and Momba District of Mbeya Region, Tanzania. They also exist in Zambia. They speak the Lambya (Ichilambya) language.

49. Luguru

The Luguru are from Pwani Region and Morogoro Region in Tanzania. They speak the Bantu Luguru language.

50. Lungu

The Lungu are a tribe of two Bantu ethnic groups: the Lungu of Chief Tafuna (Mambwe-Lungu) and the Lungu of Chief Mukupa Kaoma (Malaila-Lungu).

They live on the southwestern shores of Lake Tanganyika in Rukwa Region's Kalambo District, Tanzania, and northeastern Zambia, mainly in Mpulungu and Mbala districts. They speak the Lungu (Cilungu) language.

51. Maasai

The Maasai are a Nilotic ethnic group inhabiting northern, central, and southern Kenya and northern Tanzania, near the African Great Lakes region. They speak the Maa language.

52. Machinga

The Machinga are native to the Kilwa District of the Lindi Region on Tanzania's southern Indian Ocean coast. They speak the Machinga language.

53. Magoma

The Magoma people are based in southern Tanzania's Makete District of the Njombe Region. They speak the Magoma language.

54. Mahanji

The Mahanji are based in the Makete District of Njombe Region in southern Tanzania and reside in Kipagalo Bulongwa. They speak the Mahanji language.

55. Makonde

The Makonde are an ethnic group in southeast Tanzania, northern Mozambique, and Kenya. They speak Makonde, also known as ChiMakonde, a Bantu language closely related to Yao.

56. Makua

The Makua people, also known as Makhuwa or Wamakua, are a Bantu ethnic group found in northern Mozambique and the southern border provinces of Tanzania, such as the Mtwara Region. They speak variants of the Makua language, also called Emakua.

57. Malila

The Malila are based in southwestern Tanzania's Momba District of Songwe Region. They speak the Malila language.

58. Mambwe

The Mambwe are an ethnic group from the Mbala and Senga Hill districts of Northeastern Zambia and Kalambo District of Rukwa Region, Tanzania. They speak the Mambwe (Cimambwe) language, a dialect of Mambwe-Lungu.

59. Manda

The Manda are based in Ludewa District in the Iringa Region and northern Ruvuma Region of southern Tanzania, along the eastern shore of Lake Malawi. They speak the Manda language.

60. Manyema

The Manyema (WaManyema) are based in the Kigoma Region of Tanzania. They speak the Manyema language.

61. Matengo

The Matengo are based in Mbinga District, Ruvuma Region in southern Tanzania. Their main language is Matengo.

62. Matumbi

The Matumbi are native to Kilwa District, Lindi Region in southern Tanzania. They speak the Matumbi language.

63. Maviha

The Maviha, or Mbiha, are based along the Mtwara Region of Tanzania and Mozambique border. They speak the Mahiva language.

64. Mbugu

The Mbugu, also known as the Va'maa Ma'a, hail from the western Usambara Mountains of Lushoto District in the Tanga Region of Tanzania. They speak the Mbugu (or Ma'á) language.

65. Mbugwe

The Mbugwe are based in the Babati District of the Manyara Region and southwestern Arusha Region of Tanzania. They speak the Mbugwe language.

66. Mbunga

The Mbunga are a people from the Kilolo District of Iringa and Morogoro Regions in Tanzania. They speak the Mbunga language.

67. Meru

The Meru, also known as the Va-Rwa, are native to the slopes of Mount Meru in the Arusha Region. They speak the Kimeru language.

68. Mpoto

The Mpoto are based in the Mbinga District of Ruvuma Region in southern Tanzania, along the northeast shore of Lake Malawi. They speak the Mpoto language.

69. Mwera

The Mwera are native to the Kilwa District in the southeast Lindi Region. They speak the Mwera language.

70. Ndali

The Ndali are native to the Ileje District in Songwe Region, Tanzania and northern Malawi. They speak the Ndali language.

71. Ndamba

The Ndamba are based in south-central Tanzania. They speak the Chindamba language.

72. Ndendeule

The Ndendeule hail from Namtumbo District, Ruvuma Region, Tanzania. They speak the Ndendeule language.

73. Ndengereko

The Ndengereko hail from the southern Pwani Region, Tanzania. They speak the Ndengereko language.

74. Ndonde

The Ndonde, or Ndonde Hamba, are based in the Nachingwea District in the Lindi Region of southern Tanzania. They speak the Ndonde Hamba language, also known as Mawanda.

75. Ngasa

The Ngasa, also known as Ongamo, is based on the northeastern slopes of Mount Kilimanjaro in Rombo District, Kilimanjaro Region, Tanzania. They speak the Ngasa language.

76. Ngindo

The Ngindo are based in the southern Pwani Region, eastern Ruvuma Region, southern Morogoro Region and northern Lindi Region. They speak the Ngindo and Ndendeule languages.

77. Ngoni

The Ngoni people live in the Ravuma Region, Songea District of Tanzania. They speak the Ngoni language.

78. Ngulu

The Ngulu people, also known as the Geja, Kingulu, Nguru, Nguu, and Wayomba, hail from the Southern Kilindi District, western Tanga Region of Tanzania and Mvomero District of Morogoro Region. They speak the Ngulu language.

79. Ngurimi

The Ngurimi are based in the northern Tarime District and Serengeti District of the Mara Region of Tanzania. They speak the Ngurimi language.

80. Nindi

The Nindi are an astari putri group based in southern Tanzania, in the Songea District of Ruvuma Region. They speak the Nindi language.

81. Nyakyusa

The Nyakyusa (also called the Sokile, Ngonde, or Nkonde) live in the fertile mountains of the southern Mbeya Region of Tanzania and the northern region of Malawi. They speak the Nyakyusa language.

82. Nyambo

The Nyambo, or Ragwe, are based in the Karagwe District of Kagera Region in far northwestern Tanzania. They speak the Nyambo language.

83. Nyamwanga

The Namwanga or Nyamwanga are native to the Momba District in the Songwe Region of Tanzania, northeastern Zambia, and Northern Malawi. They speak Nyamwanga or Chinamwanga.

84. Nyamwezi

The Nyamwezi, or Wanyamwezi, are native to Tabora Region, Singida Region, Shinyanga Region and Katavi Region. They speak the Kinyamwezi language.

85. Nyanyembe

The Nyanyembe are a tribe of the Nyamwezi people of northern Tanzania. They speak the Nyanyembe language.

86. Nyaturu

The Turu (Arimi, Wanyaturu) are based in the Singida Region of north-central Tanzania. They speak the Kinyaturu language.

87. Nyiha

The Nyiha are based in the Mbeya Region, Tanzania and northeastern Zambia. They speak the Nyiha language.

88. Pangwa

The Pangwa are based in the Ludewa District of the Njombe Region in southern Tanzania. They speak the Pangwa language.

89. Pare

The Pare are indigenous to the Pare Mountains of northern Tanzania, part of the Kilimanjaro Region. They speak the Chasu or Athu language.

90. Pimbwe

The Pimbwe are based in the Rukwa Region of western Tanzania, in the neighbourhood of Mpimbwe, northwest of Lake Rukwa. They speak the Pimbwe language.

91. Pogolo

The Pogoro (also Pogolo) are based in Tanzania's Iringa and Morogoro Regions. They speak the Pogoro language.

92. Rangi

The Rangi are a Bantu-speaking ethnic group of mixed Bantu and Cushitic heritage in the Dodoma Region of central Tanzania. They speak the Rangi language.

93. Rufiji

The Rufiji people are based on the central coast of Tanzania, specifically in the Rufiji District next to the Rufiji River delta in Pwani Region. They speak the Rufiji language, which is part of the Matumbi-Makonde-Ndengereko-Yao languages.

94. Rungwa

The Rungwa are based in the Mpanda District of the Rukwa Region in western Tanzania. They speak the Rungwa language.

95. Safwa

The Safwa, or Ishisafwa, Cisafwa, and Kisafwa, are based in the mountains of the Mbeya Region, Tanzania. They speak the Safwa language, which is a member of the large Niger-Congo language family group.

96. Sagara

The Sagara (or Sagala) are based in the Kilosa District of Morogoro Region, southern Dodoma Region, and parts of Iringa Region in Tanzania. They speak the Kagulu language.

97. Sandawe

The Sandawe are based in the Dodoma Region of Tanzania. They speak the Sandawe language.

98. Sangu

The Sangu are based in the Usangu Plain of Chunya District of Mbeya Region, Tanzania. They speak the Sangu language.

99. Segeju

The Segeju are based in Tanzania's Tanga Region (particularly Mkinga District) and Kenya's Kwale County. They speak the Segeju and Digo languages.

100. Shambaa

The Shambaa people, also called the Sambaa, Shambala, Sambala or Sambara, have their ancestral home in the Usambara Mountains of Lushoto District, Korogwe District and Bumbuli District. They speak the Shambaa language.

101. Shirazi

The Shirazi, also known as Mbwera, inhabit the Swahili coast and the nearby Indian Ocean islands. They are particularly concentrated on Zanzibar, Pemba, and Comoros islands. The Shirazi speak local dialects of the Swahili language as their mother tongue.

102. Shubi

The Shubi is based in the Ngara District of the Kagera Region, Tanzania. They speak the Shubi language.

103. Sonjo

The Sonjo or Batemi hail from the northern Ngorongoro District of Arusha Region in Tanzania. They speak the Sonjo language.

104. Suba

The Suba of Tanzania lives in Rorya District, Mara Region. They speak mutually intelligible varieties of the Suba language.

105. Sukuma

The Sukuma hail from the southeastern African Great Lakes region. They are the largest and among the richest tribes in Tanzania. Their mother tongue is the Sukuma language.

106. Sumbwa

The Sumbwa are native to Bukombe District, Geita Region in central Tanzania. They speak the Sumbwa language.

107. Swahili

The Swahili people are a Bantu ethnic group primarily inhabiting the Swahili Coast, which includes the coastal areas of Kenya, Tanzania, northern Mozambique, the Comoros Islands, and Northwest Madagascar. They speak the Swahili language as their native tongue.

108. Tongwe

The Tongwe are based in Kigoma District and Uvinza District of Kigoma Region, on the eastern shore of Lake Tanganyika in western Tanzania. They speak the Tongwe language and are among the poorest tribes in Tanzania.

109. Tumbuka

The Tumbuka (or, Kamanga, Batumbuka, and Matumbuka) live in the Rukwa Region of Tanzania in Nkasi District and Sumbwanga District. They speak the Tumbuka language.

110. Vidunda

The Vidunda are based in Kilosa District of Morogoro Region, Tanzania. They speak the Vidunda language.

111. Vinza

The Vinza are based in Uvinza District of Kigoma Region, Tanzania. They speak the Vinza language, also known as Kivinza.

112. Wanda

The Wanda people are based in the Mbeya Region and Sumbawanga District of the southern Rukwa Region of Tanzania. They speak the Wanda language.

113. Wanji

The Wanji are native to the Makete District, the Kipengere Mountains of the Njombe Region, and the Mbeya Region in southern Tanzania. They speak the Wanji language.

114. Ware

The Ware people were a Bantu ethnic/ linguistic group in Tanzania that was believed to have become extinct. Their native home was on an island in the Eastern part of Lake Victoria off the coast of the Mara Region.

115. Yao

The Yao live in the southeastern regions, particularly in the Mtwara and Ruvuma Regions, east of Lake Malawi, at the southern end of Lake Malawi. They speak the Yao or Chiyao language.

116. Zanaki

The Zanaki hail from the heart of Mara Region, Tanzania, east of Lake Victoria. The group is subdivided into the Birus and the Buturis, and they speak the Zanaki language.

117. Zaramo

The Zaramo live in the coastal plains and low hills surrounding Dar es Salaam, Tanzania's capital. They speak the Zaramo language.

118. Zigua

The Zigua hail from the far northern Pwani Region and the western and southern Tanga Regions in Tanzania. They speak the Zigula language.

119. Zinza

The Zinza are native to the southwestern Islands of Lake Victoria, Tanzania. They speak the Zinza language.

120. Gujarati

The Gujarati of Tanzania are a significant minority within Tanzania's demographic landscape. Their population exceeds 60,000 individuals of Indian descent.

They reside in Dar es Salaam, Zanzibar, and other regions with significant populations. Many among them are traders and entrepreneurs.
