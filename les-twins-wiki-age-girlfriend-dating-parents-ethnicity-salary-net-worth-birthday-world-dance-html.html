We finally got our first winner for the first season of the popular dance competition show, World of Dance. And they are Les Twins!

The show crowned its first winner of the season on Tuesday night after two rounds of dance performances, where Les Twins proved to be the most deserving candidates for the million dollar grand prize.

It was a long journey of nine weeks for the top three finalists: Eva Igo, Les Twins, and Swing Latino to make up to the final round while the three judges Jennifer Lopez, Ne-Yo, and Derek Hough had to make even tougher decision in choosing the most deserving one yesterday night.

The three judges had wildly varied styles in front of them and had to decide among the Eva Igo's mature-beyond-her-eyes contemporary routines, Les Twins's out-of-this-world hip-hop performances and Swing Latino's exuberant salsa dances, then make their one of the toughest decision.

Well, after seeing the amazing performances of the finalists, they finally made their decision to crown the twins: Laurent and Larry Bourgeois as the winner of the first season.

One of the judges, Jennifer Lopez, called them powerful enough to claim the title after their final dance performance. She said:

 "The way you were moving, it was so powerful. You did Les Twins."

On the next side, it was an unbelievable moment for the twins to get awarded with the title while they managed to call it a tough competition from the very beginning and even thanked Eva for making it interesting. Their words were,

"We made it. This competition was hard from the beginning. Thank you [runner up Eva Igo] for going hard on us."

With the accomplishment of crowning the first winner, it's the end of the first season of World of Dance and probably will return to NBC for season 2 next year.

Moreover, the judge and producer of the show, Jennifer celebrated the success of the show and thus shared her experience:

 "I am so proud to not only star, but produce a show that is so close to my heart. I am so happy that World of Dance has resonated with audiences and we are able to put dancers on the pedestal they deserve. I cannot wait to continue this journey and my partnership with NBC."
