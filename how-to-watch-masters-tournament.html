Golf enthusiasts, get ready to tee off because the Masters Tournament is just around the corner. If you’re eager to catch every swing and putt without breaking the bank, you came to the right place—we’ve got you covered with the ultimate guide on how to watch the Masters Tournament live for free in 2024.

The Masters Tournament, known for its lush fairways and prestigious green jacket, traces its roots back to 1934 when it was first established by golfing icon Bobby Jones and investment banker Clifford Roberts. Since then, it has evolved into one of the most revered events in the world of golf thanks to its impressive display of skill and sportsmanship. With a prize purse of $18 million up for grabs (with $3.24 million going to the overall winner of the championship) the stakes have never been higher as the world’s top golfers vie for glory at Augusta National Golf Club.

From seasoned veterans aiming for another shot at greatness, to rising stars seeking to make their mark, the stage is already set for four days of entertaining competition.

When does the Masters Tournament air?

The 2024 Masters Tournament will be broadcast over four rounds, with the first round of the tournament airing on Thursday, April 11. Before the tournament officially kicks off, there will also be three days of pre-tournament events, featuring practice rounds beginning on Monday, April 8 and the Par 3 Contest taking place on Wednesday, April 10. How to watch the Masters Tournament live for free

The Masters Tournament will air on CBS, CBS Sports Network, ESPN and Golf Channel, which are available to stream on services like DirecTV Stream, Fubo, Peacock, and Hulu+ With Live TV. DirecTV Stream offers a five-day free trial, and starts at $79.99 per month after the trial ends. Fubo offers a seven-day free trial, and starts at $74.99 per month after the trial ends. Paramount Plus also offers a seven-day free trial and starts at $5.99 per month. While Hulu+ With Live TV doesn’t have a free trial, it is cheaper per month than some of the other options, starting at $68.99 per month, and offers plans that include free subscriptions to Disney Plus and ESPN Plus.
