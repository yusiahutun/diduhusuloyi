Professional career

Johnny Manziel started his professional career after being drafted by the Cleveland Browns in the first round of the 2014 National Football League Draft. However, his performance with the Cleveland Browns was marked by inconsistency and off-field controversies.

Despite showing flashes of talent, his time with the Browns was cut short, and he was released after the 2015 season. He only played 15 games for the Browns in the NFL. After his time in the NFL, Johnny continued his football career in other leagues.

In 2018, he played in the Canadian Football League (CFL) for the Hamilton Tiger-Cats and the Montreal Alouettes. He joined the Alliance of American Football (AAF) with the Memphis Express in 2019.

On 30 December 2020, the personality signed with Fan Controlled Football (FCF), an indoor American football league and was assigned to the Zappers. Manziel only played five games for the Zappers between 2020 and 2022. He retired from any football participation afterwards.

What happened to Johnny Manziel?

Johnny Manziel struggled with consistency, off-field controversies, and substance abuse issues. According to The Guardian, these challenges cut short his time in the NFL, leading to his departure from the Cleveland Browns after two seasons.

Despite attempts at comebacks in other football leagues, such as the CFL, AAF, and Fan-Controlled Football League, Manziel's football career was overshadowed by off-field issues and a lack of commitment.

Since then, he has transitioned from professional football to focusing on family time, golfing, entrepreneurship with his sports bar near Texas A&M, and playing football in the Fan-Controlled Football league.

Wife and children

Johnny has had a tumultuous romantic history with high-profile relationships. In 2013, he dated Sarah Savage, followed by Lauren Hanley in 2014 and Colleen Crowley in 2016, where allegations of domestic violence arose.
