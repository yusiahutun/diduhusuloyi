10p coin: What are the Royal Mints new 10p A to Z coins? What does each letter mean?

 Posted on March 30, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  625 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The Royal Mint is encouraging coin lovers to celebrate the UK “one coin at a time”.

The new series of 10p coins were released into circulation on March 1 and features an A to Z of British landmarks, icons and traditions.

From the Stonehenge, double decker bus and the NHS – people are scrambling to get their hands on the coins.

Here is all the information about the new coin collection.

The Royal Mint has released a new series of alphabet coins to celebrate everything British.

The limited release 10p coins feature 26 different symbols of “what makes Britain great” according to the Royal Mint.

Director of the Royal Mint Museum, Dr Kevin Clancy, said: “This series really drills down into the heartland of what makes Britain British.

“It’s the granularity of British life celebrated on the coinage.”

Anne Jessopp, the first female CEO of The Royal Mint, said: “These designs were selected because we feel they represent a diverse mix of elements that make up the country we all love.“

The Royal Mint website has begun selling the coins.

You can buy the whole set for £52.

You can also buy Early Strike Uncirculated coins for £2 each.

If you want to purchase a silver commemorative coin you will need to fork out £35 per piece.

The 10p coins were released into general circulation on March 5 – so coins will begin appearing in peoples’ change.

2.6 million coins were released, and the Royal Mint wants everyone to join the “Great British Coin Hunt” to try to collect all 26.

Luke Hearn, coin expert at the Change Checker website said the coins had proven to be “hugely popular”.

He said: “The fact that all 26 coins have been released into circulation is fantastic and we’ve had thousands of messages from people who are already searching for them.”

If you are serious about collecting the coins you can also pick up a presentation pack on the Royal Mint website for £9.90.

The British public were asked to vote on their favourite British symbols so they could contribute to the design of the coins.

Cricket gained 48% of the votes for the most British sport, which beat football (29%) and rugby (6%).

James Bond was voted the most British fictional character with 48% of the votes, which beat Harry Potter (11%).

British voters were in agreement that their tea was the most iconic drink (86%).

Anne Jessopp, chief executive at the Royal Mint, said: “These designs were selected because we feel they represent a diverse mix of elements that make up the country we all love.

“There is a lot to be proud of in the UK – whether it’s at the highest level, our Houses of Parliament representing democracy and freedom of speech, technological advancements such as Tim Berners-Lee’s world wide web, or just a good cup of tea.

"It’s all here in the designs."
