Emmanuel Eboue Refutes Claims He Is Living in Poverty after Divorce from British Wife

 Posted on May 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  440 words  |  Aldo Pusey

- Emmanuel Eboue has debunked claims that he is currently broke after his divorce

- The Ivorian legend claims reports circulating about his impoverished lifestyle are false

- The 37-year-old spent seven seasons with Arsenal and won trophies at Galatasaray

Former Arsenal defender Emmanuel Eboue has come out to strongly deny claims that he is living in abject poverty after divorcing his ex wife.

Back in 2017, Eboue was involved in a legal battle with ex wife Aurelie and in the process, ended up losing some of his major assets to her.

In the wake of the messy divorce, reports started emerging that Eboue was in desolate financial state and had resorted to living with friends while trying to make ends meet.

He was in particular quoted by UK outlet Sunday Mirror reportedly saying he was homeless in a publication that made headlines at the time.

The retired defender, who spent eight years at Arsenal, however, debunked the reports in a recent interview, where he reiterated that he was doing well for himself.

“When I hear people say Emmanuel Eboue lost everything, I laugh. I have properties in Ghana, I have properties in my home country (Ivory Coast).” He said as quoted by Citi Sports.“I live very comfortably and I thank God. So when people keep talking and say Emmanuel Eboue doesn’t have anything, he can’t eat, doesn’t have a place to stay, it’s not true,” he reiterated.

Eboue went on to admit that at first, he struggled after the divorce with Aurelie but has since picked himself up.

“After that [divorce case], I left and returned back to Africa. I have my own properties in Africa and so I live with that," the former player added.“When they [court] said that, I was very disappointed… To be honest, for the first time, I was very down but after that, I prayed and forgot about all of that.”

Eboue played for Arsenal for eight seasons between 2004 and 2011, having signed from Belgian outfit Beveren.

He moved on to Turkish side Galatasaray after his Arsenal stint where he won three Turkish Super Lig titles and five domestic trophies with Galatasaray.

The 37-year old got remarried to his childhood sweetheart Stephanie Boedé after returning to his home country in Ivory Coast.
