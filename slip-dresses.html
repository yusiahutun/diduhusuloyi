The best satin slip dresses of Spring 2024

Victoria Beckham x Mango

Victoria Beckham x Mango

While we love a conceal-and-reveal fashion moment, it's true that not all skin-baring cuts are easy to wear in reality. Trust Victoria Beckham to hit all the right notes: this slinky cornflower-blue dress from her anticipated Mango collection features a subtle keyhole at the chest, delivering just the right amount of drama for weddings and summer parties.

Joseph

A classic black midi slip dress comes with endless styling options. Elevate yours with strappy sandals and a tuxedo jacket in the evening, or underpin it with a turtlenneck during the day.

Galvan

A modern interpretation of Kate Moss' iconic metallic slip dress, Galvan's iridescent column is cut from foiled silk that glows in shades of green and gold. Wear it with a simple updo for an evening look with instant impact.

Arket

For something with a little more volume, turn to Arket. The brand's classic black slip is cut to flare from the waist, meaning it'll swirl beautifully as you stride. Note the flattering cowl neck and elegant straps, too.

Sezane

A breezy Parisian classic, Sezane's silk Nassima dress will look just as good with earrings and elegant sandals as it will a slouchy shirt and biker boots. Consider it a truly versatile staple you'll be reaching for for years to come.

Rabanne

Simple yet striking, Rabanne's cream satin slip is framed with chunky gold chains that fall over the arms. It's so impactful, you'll barely need jewellery.

Faithfull The Brand

Faithfull The Brand

If you've 'been there, done that' when it comes to the black slip dress, why not upgrade to this season's favourite chocolate brown shade? This satin maxi is neatly contoured around the bust, ensuring an ultra-flattering fit.

Anna October X J.Crew

Anna October X J.Crew

This sugared-almond slip is a standout piece from J. Crew's collaboration with Ukrainian designer Anna October. Designed to look just like vintage lingerie, this stretch-satin slip is finished with zigzag flat seams, picot trims and a peekaboo bra. Something sultrier? It also comes in burgundy.

Staud

Pearl-shaped silver beads are threaded along the halterneck straps on Staud's liquid-satin maxi dress. It's cut with a just-low-enough back that feels dramatic without being intimidating, and cut to fall gracefully to the floor.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Sleeper

Despite its minimalist origins, the slip dress doesn't have to be pared-back in 2024. Sleeper's signature satin dress is topped with a flourish of ostrich feathers: a fun detail that made the Ukrainian brand such a party favourite.

Asceno

Asceno is the no.1 place to go for silk slip dresses: it started out in 2014 as a nightwear brand and drops several different styles every season. With its bias-cut silhouette and maxi hemline, this onw would work perfectly layered with a bomber or dressed up with strappy sandals.

Olivia Von Halle

Olivia Von Halle

Olivia Von Halle's silk slip dress is defined by its darted cups and low V-back – this cut combined with the shell-pink hue makes it perfect for parties. In fact, the Issa design is quite the celeb favourite: we've spotted it on Angelina Jolie, Em Rata, Lily Donaldson and Rita Ora.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Ghost

Ghost is known for its simple yet strategically cut satin slip dresses. This one features thoughtful wider straps, so you can conceal a supportive bra beneath, and falls on the bias to end at the ankles. Available in a range of shades, it's a saviour for times when the dress code has you stumped.
