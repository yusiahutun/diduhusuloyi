Sister of a paedophile Dance Moms dance teacher Grant Davies prepares to testify

 Posted on February 24, 2024  |  11 minutes  |  2298 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The international world of young girls dance and in particular fans of the US TV reality TV hit show show Dance Moms will be watching with interest when Rebecca Davies takes the stand at the Royal Commission into Institutional Child Sex Abuse.

Ms Davies is expected to enter the witness box on Wednesday or Thursday to give evidence about what she knew of her brother Grant Davies' sexual abuse of their students.

As former students gave evidence claiming Rebecca Davies told students at her and her brother Grant's dance business to stay silent, a bizarre video of the siblings has emerged.

Filmed in the US just weeks before Grant Davies was arrested and charged, it shows the brother and sister with Abby Lee Miller, US 'Dance Moms' reality show TV star.

In the video Ms Miller, who is a polarising figure in the international child dance community, blames the dance students mothers for their daughters' low performance scores.

Rebecca and then Grant Davies then lecture the mothers about their negativity towards Ms Miller and coach a session to encourage 'positive energy'.

A few weeks after the video was taken, Grant Davies was charged with 63 offences against children as young as nine.

Davies was charged with indecent assault against children, aggravated sexual assault, child pornography, child grooming and acts of indecency against nine victims. Last September, he pleaded guilty to 28 offences and will be sentenced in May.

The offences were committed at his Abbotsford home and his Chiswick RG Dance Studios in Sydney's inner west, where Rebecca and Grant Davies were based, as well as other locations in NSW.

After her brother was charged, Rebecca Davies shut down RG Dance and moved to Melbourne and set up a new dance business .

In evidence so far at the Royal Commission,a former student said it was RG Studio's policy to instruct students as young as three years old to wear skimpy crop tops and shorts, with either a g-string or no underwear.

A former student identified only as BZP was among a number of young students whose parents complained to police about Davies in 2007.

BZP said Davies would make suggestive comments to students in front of his sister Rebecca Davies, a co-director of the school.

BZP told the commission that Rebecca Davies told students not to inform their parents about what happened inside the school.

"[Rebecca said], 'Anything that gets said in this classroom doesn't get taken back to your parents. It stays between these four walls'," BZM told the hearing.

Lawyer Andrew George, representing Ms Davies, told the commission his client denied telling students to stay silent.

A former student abused by her dance teacher Grant Davies (pictured) told the Royal Commission that Rebecca Davies had been aware of allegations against her brother in 2007 but let him continue teaching

Witness BZP told the commission that Rebecca Davies told students not to inform their parents about what happened inside the school run by Ms Davies and her brother Grant (pictured), but Ms Davies lawyer denied this

Rebecca Davies,  pictured coaching a dance student, travelled overseas with her brother Grant to promote RG Dance and they trained young dancers who won roles in The Lion King, Billy Elliot and Strictly Ballroom

BZP told the commission Rebecca Davies was aware of the allegations about her brother in 2007 but allowed him to continue teaching.

'[Rebecca] knew and had some idea, but she didn't take any efforts to remove him,' she said.

'I think ... the charges grew in such severity and numbers because [Davies] stayed there.' 

However, Rebecca Davies later reported her brother to police.

Rebecca Davies, who now operates her Rebecca Davies Dance Technique Conditioning Consultant studio in Melbourne, travels the world giving classes on fitness training and technique for dancers.

Rebecca Davies closed down the studio she ran with her brother when he was charged and left Sydney to open a new dance business in Melbourne

Her biography on The Dance Company says 'Rebecca Davies has revolutionised the dance industry with her conditioning programs for young dancers' and 'is constantly in high demand world-wide for her life-changing seminars which transform dancers’ techniques in a matter of minutes.'

In the business she operated with her brother, the Davies siblings trained young dancers who won roles in Billy Elliot, The Lion King and Strictly Ballroom.

Rebecca Davies also has a biography on the site of the Abby Lee Dance Studios, made famous in the reality television show 'Dance Moms'.

The programme has made an international star of young dancer Maddy Ziegler, who at just 13 years old has more than five million Instagram followers.

Rebecca and Grant Davies were guests of the show's controversial host Abby Lee Miller, who is both loved and hated by ambitious parents in the world of dance and child stardom.

Video and photos of the Davies siblings show them visiting the Abby Lee Dance studio in Pittsburgh and meeting and coaching dancers.

In the video, in which Ms Miller berates the mothers for their negative energy, she begins by introducing the siblings.

'These are my friends.,' she says. 'This is Grant and his sister Rebecca Davies. They are from Australia. They are not only dance instructors, they are motivational speakers.'

She then turns on the startled mothers and blames them for their daughters' poor scores in dance competitions.

'I have thought long and hard about why these national champions can't seem to get up to first. and I have come to the conclusion that it's all of you. It's your negativity.'

Rebecca Davies then addresses the mothers, saying: 'When you guys talking negatively about Abby, you have low energy and negative energy. These kids are going to pick up on that and that's why they're getting sevens.

 'You guys need to move your bodies like they do. You need to sit through the same training sessions that they do, in order for you to be champions like they are,' Rebecca Davies says.

The mothers look nonplussed, then don Abby Lee Dance Studios t-shirts and bright smiles as they participate in a training session.

Grant Davies then says to the girls, 'Did you feel the moment you entered the room the energy changed? Got that?'

Abby Lee Miller puts up her hand  in agreement and Grant says, 'We walk in like we're champions like we're ready to go.

'So kids, show us how it's done, we're champions we're ready to go.'

Following Grant and Rebecca Davies' return from the US trip, on which the Davies took several young Australian dancers from among their students, Mr Davies' wife found naked images of children on his computer.

She told Ms Davies and they reported the matter to police. Detectives examined the laptop and found a large number of child abuse material and evidence of child grooming.

Grant Davies was arrested and refused bail. News of the arrest caused an explosion of anger on dance blogs and social media sites across the world.

The RG Studios Facebook page, which had 8134 photos, many of them with Grant Davies with his arms around young dancers with captions like 'my gorgeous ballerina - love her', attracted a barrage of obscene comments before it was taken down.

One person wrote 'After Grant's recent appearance at Abby Lee's, even the Dance Mom kids' outfits have visibly shrunk, to keep with this deplorable trend'.

On April 9, 2013, Ms Davies issued a statement about her brother and their business, which read, 'As of, Tuesday the 9th of April, I, Rebecca Davies severed all personal and professional  contact with Grant Davies.

'Since that time he has not had any involvement with RGDance or been to the studio in Chiswick. This came about as a result of my reporting him to the police.

'I have been cooperating fully with the police and at their request have been unable to discuss the matter. A formal statement will be issued shortly after advice from my legal team.'

Ms Davies' statement attracted several hundred likes on the Facebook page, but one woman wrote 'The fact is you had this reported to you and your family 6 years ago. You did nothing then.

'You did not come to the defence of all the children that reported it. You mocked the parents that questioned it. You did not ask why so many children left in one year.' 

In the Royal Commission last week, RG's former student BZP wept and said she had viewed Grant Davies as 'a father figure'.

In evidence, she said when she was 13, Davies started to have conversations of a 'sexual nature' with her, mainly online or by text message, the Border Mail reported.

Davies repeatedly asked her whether she masturbated, telling her: 'You can try it tonight and then tell me how it feels.'

BZP told the commission: 'I felt too grossed out to tell anyone.' Davies also told the girl about a dream he had in which they had sex.

But he told her she could 'never tell anyone' about it.  BZP is now 23 and a professional dancer living in Los Angeles

Also due to give evidence to the commission is former RG Dance teacher Tracie-Marie Seipel who apparently discussed concerns about Davies with BZP's parents in February 2007 and they contacted police.

But the police investigation stalled and BZP said after she left the RG Dance school she 'had been shunned and called a liar ever since'. Ms Seipel said she had 'felt sick' when Mr Davies was not charged nine years ago.

Following Mr Davies arrest in 2013, Rebecca Davies sent personal emails to parents of RG's students saying that due to the number of clients that had withdrawn in the wake of the allegations, the business was no longer viable.

'On a personal note, my heart is breaking for all of you,' Ms Davies wrote in the emails, 'it is so devastating that one person has destroyed everything we all love.'
