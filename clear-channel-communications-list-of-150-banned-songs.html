Clear Channel Communications' List of 150 &quot;Banned&quot; Songs

 Posted on June 1, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  947 words  |  Martina Birk

In the wake of the September 11th terrorist attacks in the United States, Clear Channel Communications came under fire for an internal memo of a list of 150 "lyrically questionable" songs by everyone from the Animals to the Zombies (plus everything by Rage Against The Machine) which it "banned" its stations from playing.

Some songs are overtly violent in their intent, but the majority simply contains metaphorical language, or narrative aspects that connected uncomfortably with the Sept. 11th tragedy. Actually, when word of the list leaked to the media, CCC clarified its message that the decision to play, or not to play the listed songs was up to each individual radio station, but the intent of the memo suggested that it not play the songs.
