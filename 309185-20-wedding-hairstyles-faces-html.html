20 best wedding hairstyles for round faces

 Posted on April 21, 2024  |  12 minutes  |  2371 words  |  Martina Birk

Weddings are meant to be perfect, and when you are a woman, you can’t let something as important as your appearance ruin your day. The hairstyle is one of the things that have to be done right to complete the look. The face type should determine the hairstyle chosen. Those with round faces tend to prefer a hairstyle that gives them a slimmer look on the face. Fortunately, many awesome hairstyles suit the round face quite well. This face type usually blends well with long straight hair be it with curls or not. So take your time in selecting the best wedding hairstyles for round faces to avoid disappointments on your big day.

Maybe you have never thought about whether your face is round or not. If you find yourself in this situation, it is easy to determine if your face is round. To do this, first, you have to measure the widths of your forehead, jawline and cheeks. After that, you measure the length starting from the hairline to the tip of your chin. You have a round face if the broadest width measures the same as the length of your face. Another hint is when your jawline is soft and round as opposed to being angular.

1. The loose messy bun

The loose messy bun is a hairstyle with added height at the top. The concept for doing this is to make your face appear longer than it is. Hair is set to fall loosely on the face and in the process, makes it look slim.

2. The knotted bun

This is a favourite for women with round faces. Knotted bun hairstyle is usually big enough to make your face look smaller. It is made stylish by allowing some hair to fall on the face, thus not appearing dull. With such a hairstyle, it is impossible for people not to notice how good one looks.

3. The boho braid

It is a stunning bridal hairstyle for round face that makes one stand out from the crowd. It is long, pretty and stylish. It is made possible by making the braid loose and allowing some hair to drop on the face. The idea for doing this is to make the hair look thicker. The bride has the freedom to be creative with the boho braid such as braiding just the centre top hair only.

4. Side-swept bangs with a neat bun

Side-swept bangs with a neat bun make a round face look amazing. The bangs are swept to the side to shift from the forehead. At the same time, they add horizontal attention on the bride’s face. As a result, admirers will be focusing on the eyes and side bangs, which is a cool way to command the attention of everyone at the wedding. It is one of the perfect wedding hairstyles for plus size brides.

5. Slight pompadour bun

The slight pompadour bun with a twist is a simple but amazing soft hairstyle that completes the look on a round face. The bun has curls at the tail end, making it the highlight of the hair. The pompadour at the top is meant to add the height of the hair, which in turn makes your face gain an elongated look. Well, it is simple and candidate for casual updos for round faces.

6. Loose half braid

It is particularly a good hairstyle for individuals who are not sure whether their face is round or not. The loose half braid works perfectly for any face shape. To achieve this look, one begins by braiding the top of the hair into a fishtail braid. It is then tied halfway close to the ears using an elastic band. Roughly an inch of hair is taken from the side and rolled over the elastic band. The hair is then fluffed to add some volume. It can be a good wedding hairstyle for short hair.

7. Side swept centrefold hair

As the name suggests, the side swept centrefold hair is done by sweeping the hair to one side and folding it at the centre. Here, the braid maker is used to make loose folds before it is swept to the side. It works very well with long hair and accessories matching the outfit.

8. Folded layers

Folded layers is a simple but elegant hairdo that gives a round face a mint of sophistication. The hair is separated into sections of about one inch apart beginning from the bottom and proceeding to the top. Each section is folded in a curve, and the end is pinned up. The same procedure is done repeatedly until all sections are completed. However, the folds ought to be smaller as you progress upwards. The last side section is twisted and kept in place by a pin.

9. The French twist

The French twist bun is a hairstyle fit for any occasion. The hairdo involves twirling the hair up into a bun and then the remaining hair is tucked into the bun. It is especially a perfect choice when the time for a hairdo is limited as it can be done within a short time. When you are having trouble with hairstyles for round face thin hair should be a point of strength.

10. Curls

Curls may seem like a basic hairstyle, but it blends well with a round face. They work even better when they match the gown and accessories applied for the wedding. With curls, one has to tie or pin the hair in a loose pony. Preferring a pin will mean that each curl will have to be pinned in its place as an effort to make the hair look fuller. In tying is preferred, an elastic band is used to tie the hair in a ponytail tightly before the loose hair is fluffed up, thus giving it a thicker look.

11. The braided bun

The braided bun will take some of your time before the perfect look can be achieved. First, you will need some hairpins and elastic bands. The hair is tied up in a high ponytail leaving some side-swept hair to act as a fringe. The hair is split into equal sections, preferably three in number. The middle section is then braided in a fishtail braid and is tugged to make it lose. The same procedure is repeated for all the sections.

Take the other side and repeat the same procedure as the first but ensuring it passes over the other braid. That will be the second layer of the bun. The middle braid is rolled around the other two braids to complete the bun. It is a common Indian wedding hairstyle for round chubby face.

12. The twist and fold

The twist and fold hairstyle is done by placing a thin headband on the head around the crown. The hair is then split into three parts from the bottom with the middle section being the largest of all. The other two sections should, however, have the same width for a uniform and symmetrical look. A ponytail is tied in the middle section about 2-3 inches from the ends. The ponytail is then flipped up and folded under the headband. Keep it in place with the help of pins. The same procedure is repeated for the other section.

13. Side braids with a bun

Side braids with a bun are one of the easier hairstyles to do for your wedding. First, you will need a rat tail comb, some hairpins and elastic bands. A big section of the hair is made into a bun leaving about 2 inches only to be braided. Any bun can be used here be it a doughnut bun, a neat bun or a messy bun. Braid both sides of the hair and pin them neatly to the bun. You can add accessories that match with the hairstyle according to the occasion.

14. Cascade braids

Cascade braids cannot be made without a hairspray, a curling iron, section clips and hairpins. A curling iron is necessary only if your hair is not curly. The hair is split into three parts about an inch from the top, and then a standard braid is started. Begin from one side and pass it over the middle section. Take the other side and do the same. The middle section is then dropped. Take a part of the remaining loose hair and make it your middle section. Add some hair from the top to one side and pass it over the middle section. Do the same for the other section and drop the middle section. Keep repeating the same procedure until you reach the other side.

15. The curly bun

The curly bun is an awesome hairstyle made by tying up all the hair at the crown. The rest of the hair is divided into two halves. One half is then twisted before being pinned up halfway in the middle. The remaining hair is left to rest freely. The same is repeated for the other half. Start twisting the top portion of the hair from the halfway point and hold it in place using a pin. The ends of the three sections are then arranged neatly to make the curls at the end to be seen.

16. The French braid bun

The French braid bun does not require much and is quick to accomplish. The hair is flipped over to come to the front, and then one starts french braiding the hair from the front but stop midway. The hair is then tied into a high ponytail before being twirled into a neat bun. Use hairpins to hold it up in place.

17. The side accent braid with curls

The side accent braid with curls is as complex as its name suggests. Part some hair on the side and then braid about an inch of the side hair loosely. The braid is pulled to ensure it is loose enough. Use a pin to hold it in place. If you think it looks good enough, you can go ahead and accessorize it as it is. Alternatively, you can bun your entire hair. You will need to tie all your hair in a mid-ponytail. Use a chignon bun maker or a doughnut bun band and place it underneath the ponytail. Arrange the ponytail in a way that you do not see any part of the bun maker. Tie it in place and twirl the remaining hair around the bun and pin it. It is a typical south Indian wedding hairstyle for round chubby face.

18. The double crown braid

The crown braided double bun is a unique hairstyle that will light you up for your wedding. First, the hair is to be split diagonally to create two sections. Clip the bigger section at the top. The lower section is braided to the very end and tied using an elastic band, so it does not become loose again. The braid is then loosened with small tugs that are uniform throughout. The same is repeated for the upper bigger section. The first section is then folded into a bun at the top and pinned to remain in shape. The bun is folded flat to look like a crown. The other section is folded in the same way to have what appears like two crowns. Unlike the Indian wedding hairstyles for fuller faces, this one can fit any face type easily.

19. Simple side braid

The simple side braid is a simple hairstyle, but with the right accessories, it looks pretty nice. With this hairstyle, braiding starts from the side. A small portion of hair is reserved for the side fringe though. Hair is gradually added to the braid until it is entirely in the side braid. The braid is then loosened by pulling it slightly. Curl the side fringe with the help of a curling iron and then accessorize it with flowers and pearls.

20. The elegant bun

The elegant bun is a work of perfection and precision. The hair is split into three sections. The centre section ought to be bigger while the other two are about the same size. The bigger middle section is then tied into a high ponytail. The ponytail is lifted and a doughnut band placed underneath it using hair pins. The ponytail is then pulled down. Hair is arranged to cover the doughnut band from the top. An elastic band is used to hold it in place. Merge the loose hair with the lower ponytail by folding it. Use a pin to hold it tight and let the rest of the hair to match with the middle section.

Try the best wedding hairstyles for round faces above before your big day to decide on one that appeals the most. It would even be better if you tried the hairstyles with your gown to get an idea of what you will look like to avoid any surprises. Doing all the above can only mean that you will be left with one more thing to do, accessorize! Ensure that your accessories match with the hairstyle and gown chosen for the occasion. With all that done, the rest should be a happily ever after in your married life.
