While nature has the best exfoliators in the form of walnut scrubs, bitter gourd seed powder, etc., a major drawback about them is their rough and coarse texture. To truly refresh and rejuvenate their skin, one requires a gentler exfoliant. While salicylic acid may be present in nature, in its purest form, using it directly is not the best idea. Hence, it is always advisable to use a carefully formulated salicylic acid body wash to cater to the needs of our skin. The best salicylic acid body washes often contain other nourishing and potent ingredients that not only exfoliate dead skin cells and grime but also preserve your skin's natural oils while making it healthy, soft, and smooth. As a result of adding them to your routine, you’ll achieve a clear canvas that’s free from dirt, excess sebum, dead skin cells, and debris.

According to dermatologist Dr. Jushya Sarin “Salicylic acid fights the existing structure of dead skin cells, and accumulates sebum on your skin. However, the average lifetime of a skin cell is up to 1-2 weeks. (Hence), it is advisable not to use a salicylic acid body wash on a regular basis. Just the way over-washing or over-scrubbing metal surfaces can commence a cycle of deterioration, exfoliating one's skin regularly can be precarious too.” Hence, it is important to mollycoddle your skin with moisturizing agents to alleviate the abrasive action.

    How to Choose the Best Salicylic Acid Body Wash?

    While skin-related concerns continue to increase at an elevated rate here are a few factors that you can take into consideration before selecting the appropriate salicylic body wash as per your skin type. 

    1. Percentage of Salicylic Acid - The percentage of salicylic acid in the body wash or the soap makes a huge difference. Salicylic acid has a strong penetrative property that helps unclog your pores. However, the percentage to look for in your body wash varies as per the skin type. If you have thick, oily or acne-prone skin, a higher percentage of exfoliants will come in handy to deep cleanse it and prevent blemishes. But for dry and sensitive skin, a smaller concentration goes a long way. It is always advised to consult a dermatologist to understand what works best for your skin. 

    2. Moisturizing Properties  - As we are aware salicylic acid body washes act as an exfoliant that helps and facilitates the regeneration of cells, newly emerged skin cells that are in their nascent stage need a strong lipid constituting aid to replenish their barrier. Therefore, one should always opt for a body wash with salicylic acid that also has a fair percentage of humectants and emollients that replenishes the skin's natural oils and should also regulate sebum production. 

    3. Texture and Scent: The texture of a salicylic acid body wash is as important as the ingredients. It should be easily absorbent and not very abrasive. A smooth textured salicylic acid body wash will gently commence the exfoliation without being too harsh on the skin. The scent and fragrance of the salicylic acid body wash should be mild and not too overpowering for an overall pleasant bathing experience. 

    How to Use a Salicylic Acid Body Wash?

    Step 1: Wet your skin with warm water, lukewarm as it will dilate the blood vessels and increase blood flow to the skin cells 

    Step 2: Take a generous amount of your body wash onto your loofah and gently lather over your skin. 

    Step 3: You can take a larger amount for the rough areas like letting the body wash later 

    Step 4: Allow the lather to sit for a while (1-2 minutes ) on the skin to allow the exfoliation to take place properly. 

    Step 5: Wash it off with lukewarm water and follow it up with a ceramide-based moisturizer for best results. 

    Benefits of Using A Salicylic Body Wash

    Salicylic body washes come with an array of benefits, a few of which are mentioned below:

    1. Combats Acne

    Salicylic body washes work to battle acne by penetrating deep within the pores and dissolving excess oil. This prevents any further breakouts from occurring on the body.

    2. Exfoliates the Skin

    The exfoliating properties in salicylic acid get rid of dead skin cells, giving the user smoother skin and promoting a more bright and even tone.

    3. Improves Skin Texture

    When used consistently, a salicylic acid body wash can enhance the skin texture by promoting the shedding of dead cells, leaving it much softer and more refined-looking.

    4. Prevents Clogged Pores

    The salicylic acid that's present in the body wash prevents the pores from getting clogged, thus preventing acne, blackheads, and whiteheads from appearing.

    5. Reduces Inflammation And Redness

    The anti-inflammatory properties of salicylic acid are known to soothe and calm skin that's irritated, which in turn reduces the appearance of redness and inflamed areas.

    6. Regulates Oil Production

    By balancing out the skin's oil levels, salicylic acid helps control sebum, minimizing the blockage of pores, and promoting healthier skin.

    Sharing the conditions when one should avoid using a salicylic acid body washes, our contributor, Dr. Harikiran says, “There are certain instances where it is best to avoid using a salicylic acid body wash. If you have open wounds or cuts on your skin, it is recommended to wait until they have healed before using a salicylic acid body wash. The exfoliating properties of salicylic acid can further irritate the affected area and slow down the healing process. It is also important to avoid using salicylic acid body washes on skin that is sunburned or has recently been waxed, as this can cause further irritation.”

    FAQ's

    Can I use salicylic acid body wash every day?

    It is not advisable to use a salicylic acid body wash daily as the lifetime of a skin cell is limited up to 2- 3 weeks and excessive usage of salicylic acid body washes can lead to irritation, dryness and flaky skin.

    How long does it take salicylic acid body washes to work?

    The effective results of a salicylic acid body wash can be only witnessed through repeated and routine usage, for best results, it is advisable to use a product for up to 6 weeks.

    Who should use salicylic acid body washes?

    Salicylic acid washes work great for individuals with oily and acne-prone skin as they help in exfoliation and unclogging pores. However, there are salicylic body washes that cater to different skin types.

    Are there any side effects of using salicylic acid body washes?

    Some possible side effects of using salicylic acid body washes are dryness, irritation, and peeling of the skin.

    Can salicylic acid body washes be used on all body parts?

    Though salicylic acid body washes are generally considered safe to be used on all body parts, you should avoid using them on areas such as the eyes or mucous membranes that can be very sensitive.

    Should I moisturize after using a salicylic acid body wash?

    Yes, it’s recommended that you moisturize so that you can counteract any potential dryness and keep your skin supple and hydrated.

    Can a salicylic acid body wash reduce acne scars?

    Though salicylic acid body washes work to improve the texture of the skin and reduce acne, they may not be very effective in getting rid of acne scars. There are other treatments available for this.

    Are salicylic acid body washes suitable for dry skin?

    Most salicylic body washes may be too drying for dry skin types. It is better if you avoid them or look for ones that are specifically tailored to dry skin.

    What’s better for acne, salicylic acid or glycolic acid?

    Salicylic acid works better at unclogging pores in skin that’s oily and prone to acne. On the other hand, glycolic acid acts as an exfoliant and is best for treating hyperpigmentation.

    Is salicylic acid safe to use during pregnancy?

    It is generally considered safe but we suggest that you consult with a healthcare professional first to get more personalized advice.
