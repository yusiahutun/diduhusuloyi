President Uhuru Kenyatta has officially unveiled the Uhuru Gardens National Monument and Museum ahead of today’s Wednesday, June 1, Madaraka Day National celebrations to be held at the same venue.

Uhuru hailed the multi-agency team that worked for 21 months to deliver the project that showcases Kenya’s history.

The head of state arrived at the venue on Tuesday, May 31, at 5pm for the official opening and unveiling of the iconic facility.

The facility on a 68-acre piece of land along Lang’ata Road in Nairobi was once a detention centre during the struggle for independence.

After unveiling the facility, Uhuru, accompanied by his visiting Sierra Leone counterpart, Julius Maada Bio, made an extensive tour of the ultra-modern museum developed by the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF).

12,300 artefacts on display

The museum directors said they had collected 12,300 artefacts from different parts of the country showcased at the museum.

The artefacts displayed in the Hall of Martyrs are meant to honour people who died during the struggle for independence.

It also has a military gallery showcasing what Kenya has been able to achieve in terms of weapons, and ammunition, all on display so that all the generations can see what Kenya has been able to work with to safeguard its borders.

“Among the outstanding attractions at the new Uhuru Gardens National Monument and Museum is the military gallery with a rich display of Kenya Defence Forces weaponry, equipment and technology through the decades,” reads a statement from State House Kenya.

There are also artefacts from former presidents Jomo Kenyatta, Daniel Moi and Mwai Kibaki, all on display.

Uhuru's final Madaraka Day fete

On Friday, May 27, Government Spokesperson Cyrus Oguna invited Kenyans to President Uhuru Kenyatta's final Madaraka Day fete.

Oguna termed the 2022 Madaraka Day celebrations a special one.

“It will be a special one as it’s the last Madaraka Day that His Excellency President Uhuru Kenyatta will officiate in his term as president,” Oguna said.

He spoke at Uhuru Gardens along Lang’ata Road in Nairobi, where the celebrations will be hosted.
