Meaning of She Calls Me Back by Noah Kahan

 Posted on August 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  570 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The Noah Kahan song 'She Calls Me Back' is an emotionally charged track that explores themes of love, connection, and the fear of vulnerability. The lyrics paint a picture of two individuals who share a profound connection, but are grappling with their own insecurities and uncertainties.One standout lyric in the song is "Does it bite at your edges? Do you lie awake restless?" These lines evoke a sense of unease and inner turmoil. It suggests that the characters in the song are unable to fully let go of their fears and anxieties, causing their relationship to be filled with tension and discomfort. This lyric helps develop the theme of vulnerability and the fear of being hurt. It highlights how the characters struggle to open themselves up completely, fearing the potential pain that comes with love and emotional intimacy.Another significant lyric in the song is "This town's the same as you left it, Your page was blank, but I read it." These lines hint at longing, regret, and a sense of missed opportunities. The characters seem to be haunted by the past and the choices they made. This lyric helps develop the theme of nostalgia and the desire to return to a time when things were simpler. It reflects the characters' yearning for a chance to rewrite their story and make amends for any mistakes they may have made.The chorus of the song, "Oh, there was Heaven in your eyes, I was not baptized, Everything's alright when She calls me back, she calls me back," emphasizes the sense of comfort and relief that the characters find in each other's presence. It suggests that their connection is a source of solace and a sanctuary from the hardships of life. This chorus helps develop the theme of love as a healing force that brings peace and stability to their lives.Kacey Musgraves' verse in the song adds another layer to the narrative. Her lyrics, "I'm runnin' out of tears to cry, They’re gone before they hit my cheeks," convey a sense of emotional exhaustion. It hints at the character's struggle with processing and expressing their emotions. This lyric helps develop the theme of emotional resilience and the ability to weather hardships. It showcases the characters' determination to overcome their internal struggles and find strength in their connection with each other.The bridge of the song, "All the pain I should have saved, Oh, I was too afraid of, Livin' life in your footsteps, In your heart or in your head," delves into the fear of vulnerability and the consequences of holding back. It suggests that the characters have been guarding themselves from fully embracing the relationship due to past experiences or personal fears. This bridge helps develop the theme of emotional growth and the realization that sometimes one must take risks and face their deepest fears in order to experience true love and connection.In conclusion, 'She Calls Me Back' by Noah Kahan is a song that explores the complexities of love and the fear of vulnerability. Through its poignant lyrics, the song delves into themes of emotional resilience, missed opportunities, and the healing power of love. It serves as a reminder that sometimes, in order to find happiness and fulfillment, one must confront their fears and allow themselves to be vulnerable. Ultimately, the song encourages listeners to take risks, open their hearts, and embrace the possibility of finding connection and love in their lives.
