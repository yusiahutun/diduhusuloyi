State House Auction: List of Vehicles Kenya Gov't Wants to Sell for KSh 110k to KSh 1.3m

 Posted on May 4, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  578 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • The office of the president announced the sale of used vehicles, tyres and batteries, among other items, through a public auction
  • State House Comptroller, in collaboration with Astorion Auctioneers, invited bidders ahead of the auction on Friday, June 16
  • Among the vehicles to be auctioned include a Toyota van at a cost of KSh 110,000 and a Range Rover at KSh 1.25 million
  • Gerishon Mjomba from the Comptroller of State House Office confirmed bidders are required to pay KSh 50,000, refundable to those who lose the bid

President William Ruto's office is selling used vehicles and other items.

The office of the State House Comptroller published an auction notice to the public, seeking prospective bidders for unserviceable motor vehicles, tyres and assorted scrap metals.

State House Nairobi auction

The auction conducted by Astorion Auctioneers set conditions for bidders, including a refundable deposit of KSh 50,000.

Gerishon Mjomba, an officer from the State House Comptroller Office, said the deposit is only refundable to losing bids.

"The KSh 50k deposit is only refundable to bidders who will not win. Winners will not be refunded and are required to pay the item price," Mjomba told TUKO.co.ke in a phone interview.

Mjomba revealed that the cars and other items are in condition for repair, and any individual who acquires them can service them and ready them for use.

"When we say unserviceable, we mean the vehicles are in a condition that can be repaired... but it is expensive for us at the government. Anyone who buys them can repair them," he said.

State House auction terms

Successful bidders will be required to collect their items within 30 days or pay a storage fee of KSh 1,000 per day.

Taxes and other charges resulting from the transfer of the items will be borne by the purchaser.

"The declared purchaser (highest bidder) MUST pay 25% of the purchase price in cash for the lots bided, and the balance must be paid in cash within seven (7) days from the Auction day, failure to which the initial deposit will be forfeited and the item offered to the second highest bidder," read the catalogue in part.

This followed the National Treasury's budget allocation for new cars in the executive office.

Treasury CS Njuguna Ndung'u set aside KSh 802.2 million to buy new motor vehicles for President William Ruto, his deputy Rigathi Gachagua and Prime Cabinet Secretary Musalia Mudavadi.
