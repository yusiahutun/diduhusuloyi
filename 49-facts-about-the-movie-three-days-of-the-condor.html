Three Days of the Condor is a gripping thriller that has captivated audiences since its release in 1975. Directed by Sydney Pollack and starring Robert Redford, the film tells the story of Joseph Turner, a CIA analyst who finds himself caught in a web of conspiracy after his entire office is brutally murdered. As Turner races against time to uncover the truth, he becomes entangled in a dangerous game of cat and mouse, unsure of who to trust.

With its suspenseful plot, memorable performances, and thought-provoking themes, Three Days of the Condor has earned its place as a classic in the world of espionage cinema. “Three Days of the Condor” was released in 1975.

Directed by Sydney Pollack, this political thriller captivated audiences with its intense storyline and stellar performances.

The film is based on the novel “Six Days of the Condor” by James Grady.

The screenplay was adapted by Lorenzo Semple Jr. and David Rayfiel, staying true to the thrilling essence of the original story.

The movie stars Robert Redford in the lead role.

Redford delivers a captivating performance as Joseph Turner, a CIA analyst who becomes the target of assassination after stumbling upon a conspiracy.

Faye Dunaway plays the female lead, Kathy Hale.

Dunaway brings depth and vulnerability to her character as she finds herself entangled in the dangerous world Turner is caught up in.

Max von Sydow portrays the enigmatic assassin known as Joubert.

Von Sydow’s portrayal of Joubert is chilling and memorable, adding to the suspense and intrigue of the film.

The movie explores themes of government conspiracy and espionage.

As Turner tries to uncover the truth behind the assassination plot, he uncovers a web of lies and deceit within his own organization.

The iconic line, “I’m not really interested in what you did before. I don’t care about your friends. I don’t care about your politics,” is spoken by Joubert.

This line perfectly encapsulates the mysterious and ruthless nature of Joubert’s character.

FAQs

1. Who directed Three Days of the Condor?
Three Days of the Condor was directed by Sydney Pollack. He is known for his work on other acclaimed films such as Tootsie and Out of Africa.

2. When was the movie released?
The movie was released on September 24, 1975.

3. Is Three Days of the Condor based on a true story?
No, Three Days of the Condor is not based on a true story. It is a fictional suspense thriller written by James Grady and adapted for the screen by Lorenzo Semple Jr. and David Rayfiel.

4. Who stars in Three Days of the Condor?
The movie stars Robert Redford as the protagonist, Joseph Turner, along with Faye Dunaway, Cliff Robertson, and Max von Sydow in pivotal roles.

5. What is the running time of the movie?
Three Days of the Condor has a running time of approximately 1 hour and 58 minutes.
