1. Brownsville, Brooklyn

Brownsville, Brooklyn, is considered one of the worst neighborhoods in New York due to its high crime rates, poverty levels, and other social issues.

In 2022, the serious crime rate in Brownsville was 18.9 serious crimes per 1,000 residents, significantly higher than the citywide average of 14.2 serious crimes per 1,000 residents.

Brownsville is also among New York ghetto areas. Its median household income is $37,320, lower than the citywide average of $50,473. Furthermore, it has a high unemployment rate, with 18.02% of the population unemployed.

2. Soundview, South Bronx

Soundview in the South Bronx is considered a challenging neighborhood in New York due to various factors such as crime rates and poverty levels. The rate of violent crime in Soundview is 4.130 per 1,000 residents during a standard year.

In 2022, the serious crime rate in Parkchester/Soundview was 18.4 serious crimes per 1,000 residents, compared to 14.2 citywide. Also, the chance of being a victim of violent crime in the Bronx is 1 in 90, and property crime is 1 in 31.

A crime occurs every 1 hour and 37 minutes on average in Soundview, making homes 300% more likely to be robbed.

3. East Harlem

East Harlem is considered one of the worst neighborhoods in New York due to various factors, including high crime rates and poverty levels. In 2022, the poverty rate in East Harlem was 31.2%, significantly higher than the citywide average of 18.0%.

4. Bedford-Stuyvesant (Bed-Stuy), Brooklyn

Bedford-Stuyvesant (Bed-Stuy) is considered a challenging neighborhood in New York because the crime rate in Bed-Stuy is 24% higher than the national average, with violent crime being 175% above average.

The poverty rate in Bed-Stuy is 24.2%, which is higher than the citywide median household income of $72,150. The unemployment rate in Bed-Stuy is 17.94%, which is also higher than the citywide average.

These statistics indicate that Bed-Stuy is a neighborhood with high crime rates and poverty levels, making it a less desirable place to live.

5. Jamaica, Queens

Jamaica, Queens, is known for its crime rates and safety issues. The rate of murder in Jamaica is 0.0154 per 1,000 residents during a standard year. Also, Jamaica has high rates of violent crimes such as assault, robbery, and theft.

In comparison to national averages, Jamaica's crime rates exceed national averages, with a higher chance of being a victim of violent or property crime compared to other communities in America.

6. Mott Haven, The Bronx

Mott Haven, The Bronx, is considered one of the worst neighborhoods in New York due to its high crime rates and poverty levels. The poverty rate in Mott Haven was 36.6% in 2021, significantly higher than the citywide rate of 18.0%.

Also, the serious crime rate in Mott Haven was 28.0 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2022, which is notably high compared to other areas.

Approximately 43% of Mott Haven residents live below the Federal Poverty Level, making it the third-poorest neighborhood in NYC. The poverty level has contributed significantly to the high crime rate in the area.

7. Hunts Point, The Bronx

Hunts Point has a history of crime and poverty, with almost half of its population living below the poverty line. The neighborhood has been associated with the drug trade, leading to a significant presence of drug addicts and a notorious prostitution industry since the 1980s.

Fort Greene/Clinton Hill in Brooklyn is not necessarily the worst neighborhood in New York, but it does face some challenges. Which borough in NYC has the highest crime rate?

The borough in New York City with the highest crime rate is The Bronx. It grapples with various challenges, including safety concerns and subpar housing conditions.

The Bronx consistently ranks high in reported violent crimes, making it an area demanding vigilance. The neighborhood faces property crime issues as well. While it is not the absolute worst, caution is still warranted.

Is Queens safer than Brooklyn?

Crime statistics show Queens is safer than Brooklyn, the Bronx, and even Manhattan. While Brooklyn and Manhattan may feature pricier real estate, Queens boasts a violent crime rate approximately 40% lower than Brooklyn and 45% lower than Manhattan.

Is Bronx dangerous?

The Bronx is known for its high crime rate, making it one of the most dangerous boroughs in New York City. It has the highest rate of violent crime in the city, which is nearly three times higher than in Queens and Staten Island.
