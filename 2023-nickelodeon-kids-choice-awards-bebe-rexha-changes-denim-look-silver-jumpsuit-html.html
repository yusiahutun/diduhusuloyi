Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards: Bebe Rexha changes from all-denim look to silver jumpsuit

 Posted on June 17, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1187 words  |  Martina Birk

Bebe Rexha left fans mesmerized with her latest fashion choices at the 2023 Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards in Los Angeles on Saturday.

Before performing in a shiny silver ensemble to the lively crowd, the In the Name of Love singer, 33, arrived to the show in a bedazzled jean jumpsuit with an open neckline as she arrived at the event before hitting the stage as the main performer of the night.

Her jumpsuit was decked out from head to toe in silver gems, and flaunted her figure perfectly.

It also featured a flare cut at her legs and short sleeves. She also had the top unzipped to the middle of her chest.

The blonde beauty accessorized with several rings and large white heart-shaped earrings.

She wore her hair down and parted in the middle with layers cut to frame her face.

For makeup, she went for a pink look, with rosy cheeks and pink lipgloss.

Later as the three-time Grammy nominee performed her hit song I'm Good, which was nominated at the 2023 Grammy Awards for Best Dance/Electronic Recording, Rexha slipped into another jumpsuit. 

Following her performance, the star's second look got a bright green surprise as she was hit with the famous Nickelodeon slime. 

The green goo came from the sides of the stage and covered Rexha and her dancers from head to toe.  

Rexha was nominated for her first Kids' Choice Award in 2019. She was up for Favorite Collaboration with Florida Georgia Line for their song Meant to Be.

They lost to DJ Khaled, Justin Bieber, Chance the Rapper, and Quavo for their song No Brainer.

The Nickelodeon Kids' Choice Awards will air live from the Microsoft Theater in Los Angeles for the first time on Saturday.

The 34th annual show will be co-hosted by sports analyst Nate Burleson, 41, and Dancing with the Stars winner and TikTok star Charli D’Amelio, 18. 

Adam Sandler, 56, will receive the King of Comedy Award at the ceremony. 

The event will feature appearances from Melissa McCarthy, Pete Davidson, Halle Bailey, Awkwafina, Seth Rogen, Michelle Rodriguez, and Chris Pine, among others.

Elizabeth Olsen, Letitia Wright, Lupita Nyong’o, Millie Bobbie Brown, Natalie Portman and Sarah Jessica Parker are up for the favorite movie actress award.

Meanwhile Chris Hemsworth, Chris Pratt, Dwayne Johnson, Jim Carrey, Ryan Reynolds and Tom Cruise are up for favorite movie actor.

Nominees for favorite family TV star are Hilary Duff, Jenna Ortega, Millie Bobby Brown, Miranda Cosgrove, Sadie Sink and Tracee Ellis Ross. 

The men in the category are Caleb McLaughlin, Finn Wolfhard, Gaten Matarazzo, Jerry Trainor, Ewan McGregor, and Ralph Macchio. 

D'Amelio joked about her role as a co-host of the show in a press release: 'I got slimed at the KCAs in 2021, saw my sister get slimed at last year's show and now, no one is safe with me as co-host.'
