Education is a vital human right that enhances human welfare and the standard of life. It is a fundamental instrument for accelerating economic and social growth. In 2017, the government launched a Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) via the Ministry of Education to teach holistic learners with 21st-century abilities for the labour market. However, there have been several pertinent and contemporary issues in CBC implementation.

A competency-based curriculum centres around particular learning procedures identifying students' abilities or skills. In addition to providing a more personalised path to course completion, the competency-based approach is also organised to show learning in clearly specified capabilities. Challenges that the CBC has encountered in its implementation

CBC is a fascinating program that can enhance educational performance in Kenya and abroad. However, it calls for rigorous coordination, planning, assessment, monitoring, and improvement to assure its efficacy and sustainability. The following are some of the challenges facing CBC in Kenya.

1. Lack of adequate teaching-learning materials

CBC necessitates more resources than standard curricula, including textbooks, instructional materials, infrastructure, and technology. Many Kenyan schools need more resources, particularly in rural and marginalised regions. This impacts the quality and efficacy of CBC delivery and evaluation.

2. Inadequate awareness and support

CBC is a significant departure from the old curriculum and needs the participation and backing of all stakeholders, particularly parents, students, community members, businesses, and lawmakers.

However, many participants are not aware of or persuaded of the benefits and reasoning of CBC and experience difficulties transitioning to the new system. This may result in opposition or complacency towards CBC implementation.

3. Large class size and inadequate number of teachers

The execution of the CBC in Kenyan schools requires the provision of an infrastructure development programme money to allow for more classrooms to meet the rising number of students, as well as the rationalisation of the number of instructors to learners and the recruitment of more teachers.

4. Inadequate training of teachers on CBC

The primary school teachers stated that they accepted CBC but could not correctly administer it due to a lack of necessary information and skills to apply the curriculum. Teachers must be taught and supported to adopt CBC efficiently and confidently.

5. Assessment standardisation

One of the issues that CBC has in higher education is standardising the procedures used to assess competence or skills. This is because, as a personalised learning strategy, CBC focuses on topic mastery regardless of location, time, or road to completion.
