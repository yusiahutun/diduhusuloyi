10 best black male stand-up comedians of all time

 Posted on May 30, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1414 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Making people laugh seems like the easiest job out there, but any comedian will tell you that it is not. A good comedian has to be adaptable because different audiences react to jokes in different ways. The best black male stand-up comedians can adapt to different audiences and tell jokes without offending anyone.

Comedy remains a world still dominated by men even with the numerous efforts aimed at achieving equality for all genders. There is progress but more needs to be done especially by the established male comedians in giving their female counterparts a hand. Identity is the key for most of the best black male comedians to remain relevant in a fast-changing world.

10 best black male stand-up comedians

A good comedian has to do research on the kind of jokes to tell and how to say them. At the same time, he has to be sensitive to the feelings of both the audience and subjects of his jokes. Here is the list of 10 best black male stand-up comedians:

1. Dave Chappelle

The bald black comedian has been around the block for a while. He is 47 years old and not about to stop. The star has worked as an actor, writer and producer. Being a comedian is what put him on the map and what he probably enjoys doing most.

He used to have his comedy sketch series, Chappelle’s Show between 2003 and 2006. He quit in the middle of production and returned to stand-up comedy. Few black stand-up comedians are as decorated as Chappelle.

He is a recipient of several awards such as four Emmy Awards, three Grammy Awards and the Mark Twain Prize. Chappelle was once referred to as a “comic genius” by Esquire. In 2017, he was ranked at number 9 in the 50 Best Stand-Up Comics of All Time by Rolling Stone.

2. Richard Pryor

Richard Pryor died in 2005 at the age of 65. However, he is still regarded as one of the best black male stand-up comedians who ever lived. He was able to attract a large audience thanks to his trenchant observations and comedic storytelling.

Most of his work is made up of concert movies and recordings. Some of his accolades include an Emmy Award, 5 Grammy Awards, and 2 American Academy of Humor awards. He was also listed as the greatest stand-up comedian of all time in 2017.

3. Eddie Murphy

There cannot be mention of the names of black comedians without Eddie Murphy in the list. He started as a comedian but later diversified into acting, writing and singing. His breakthrough came on the sketch comedy show Saturday Night Live.

Murphy started listening to Richard Pryor’s comedy albums when he was 15 years old. He was also influenced by Bill Cosby, Robin Williams and Redd Foxx. In 2020, he won the Outstanding Guest Actor in a Comedy Series.

4. Chris Rock

Apart from being a comedian, Chris Rock is also an actor, filmmaker and television producer. He started as a stand-up comedian and actor but his role in Saturday Night Live in the early 1990s brought him mega-stardom.

One of his important works is the sitcom Everybody Hates Chris (2005-2009) based on his life which he developed, wrote and narrated. He has also starred in several successful films such as:

  • Down to Earth (2001)
  • Head of State (2003)
  • The Longest Yard (2005)
  • The Madagascar (2005-2012)

Rock’s accomplishments as one of the funniest black comedians are endless both in stand-up comedy and beyond. He has hosted the Academy Awards twice. He has also been the recipient of four Emmy Awards and three Grammy Awards.

Comedy Central conducted a poll in which Rock was voted as the fifth-greatest stand-up comedian. He was voted as the ninth-greatest stand-up comic on Channel 4’s 100 Greatest Stand-Ups in 2007.

5. Bernie Mac

Bernie Mac was an American comedian, actor and voice actor. He teamed up with other comedians such as Cedric the Entertainer, Steve Harvey and D. L. Hughley to make the film The Original Kings of Comedy. He also a had a little stint hosting the HBO show Midnight Mac.

Mac starred in his eponymous show that aired from 2001 to 2006. It earned him two Emmy Award nominations for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series. Mac died in 2008 at the age of 50.

6. Redd Foxx

Redd Foxx was an American stand-up comedian and actor famous for his nightclub acts in the mid-1950s and 1960s. He was nicknamed "King of the Party Records" because of his deeds. He influenced most of today’s comedians and pop culture as well through his catchphrases, body language and facial expressions.

Foxx performed in more than 50 records in his lifetime. In 2004, the Comedy Central Presents: 100 Greatest Stand-Ups of All Time ranked him as the 24th. He portrayed Fred G. Sanford in the television show Sanford and Son for which he received a Golden Globe Award.

7. Martin Lawrence

Martin Fitzgerald Lawrence is one of the most famous American stand-up comedians. He was born in Frankfurt, Hesse, West Germany. Apart from stand-up comedy, the star is also an actor, producer, writer, talk show host and even a former Golden Gloves boxer.

His breakthrough came in the 1990s as he became an instant hit in Hollywood. Lawrence started doing comedy shows when he was in Washington, DC. He has won several awards including Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series twice and the BET Comedy Award.

8. Katt Williams

Micah Sierra Williams, popularly known as Katt Williams is an American comedian. He is also a rapper, singer and actor. The comedian emancipated himself from his parents when he was 13 and relocated to Florida, where he supported himself as a street vendor.

The star started performing locally at his Cincinnati neighbourhood. After perfecting his delivery, he started to perform his routine in clubs nationwide. Within a short time, he had appeared on stages such as The Improv, The Comedy Club, The Ice House and The Hollywood Park Casino.

9. Chris Tucker

Chris has been a stand-up comedian since the 1990s when he started performing on Def Comedy Jam. He is famous for his role as Detective James Carter from the Rush Hour film series. He learned from an early age that humour had the power of drawing attention. He hence sought a career as a comedian instead of joining his father’s janitorial business.

Upon finishing high school, Tucker moved from Decatur to Los Angeles to become a comedian and actor. He became the most famous black stand-up comedians after making impressions of Eddie Murphy and Richard Pryor.

10. Kevin Hart

Talk of a short black comedian and Kevin Hart’s name will pop up. The 41-year-old American comedian comes from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He launched his career by winning several amateur comedy competitions at clubs in New England.

His success as an amateur comedian enabled him to be featured in Undeclared. In 2008, he released his first stand-up album I’m a Grown Little Man which soared him to great heights in the comedy world. Hart has been the recipient of several awards such as:

  • The BET Award’s Best Actor for Kevin Hart: Laugh at My Pain
  • NAACP Image Award’s Outstanding Comedy Series for Real Husbands of Hollywood
  • NAACP Image Awards’ Entertainer of the Year
  • Teen Choice Awards’ Choice Comedian
