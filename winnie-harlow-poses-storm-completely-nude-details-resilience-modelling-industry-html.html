Winnie Harlow poses up a storm completely nude as she details resilience in the modelling industry

 Posted on September 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  496 words  |  Martina Birk

Winnie Harlow looked sensational this week as she posed up a storm in a brand new cover photoshoot for Women's Health, stripping down for the shots.

The Canadian model, 28, showed off her camera credentials while going completely nude for the pictures, kicking back in the great outdoors.

And talking with the publication, the star, whose real name is Chantelle Whitney Brown-Young, revealed the inspiration behind being known by name Winnie.

She also touched on dealing with rejection in the industry and how she has managed to storm her way to the top of the industry despite battles.

'I got a lot of nos along the way, but I just pushed through,' explained the covergirl, who suffers from skin condition vitiligo.

She gained prominence in 2014 as a contestant on the 21st cycle of the U.S. television series America's Next Top Model. 

But before being known as top model Winnie, the star was born Chantelle and decided to come up with a nickname for her Facebook profile as a teenager.

Drawing on her love for Winnie the Pooh and Marilyn Monroe, whose inspiration was Jean Harlow, she came up with her now iconic moniker.

But it wasn't a breeze getting to where she is, with the star telling Women's Health: 'My younger self always believed in the power of faith, strength, and perseverance, while learning vulnerability and being authentically myself. 

Adding: 'I got a lot of nos along the way, but I just pushed through. To achieve my goals with the world watching has been scary at times, but all in all, the ultimate blessing.'

Taking to the cover of the magazine, Winnie only donned a pair of white trainers while going completely naked - showing off her stunning figure. 

Her lengthy locks were left to fall freely in a naturally tousled curl, as they were pushed over to the side in a textured style. 

After once being bullied for her vitiligo, the beauty is now a trailblazer to young people and embraces her body and skin.

Adding on the empowerment this gives, she shared: 'It’s beautiful that I can help inspire confidence in people, no matter what walk of life they are from'. 

While the continued that it took her 'time' to accept her body as it is whilst growing up and learning to love her condition.

In 2016, Winnie made history when she became the first model with vitiligo to walk in the Victoria's Secret Fashion Show. 

Since then, she has dominated the catwalk for a number of fashion houses while gracing glossy magazine covers.
