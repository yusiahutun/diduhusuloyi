How to become a pilot in Kenya: Requirements, schools, fees, salary

Almost every little child's dream is to become a pilot once they grow up. Somehow, that dream is never actualized by many as very few make it to the profession, leaving the demand for captains very high. It is rare to find an unemployed pilot, and that can only be a good sign; that picking this profession guarantees employment. On the flip side, there is little information on how to become a pilot in Kenya, which could partly be why many give up on their dream.

Wanting to be an aircraft captain in Kenya is a journey not only of passion but also a range of requirements and qualifications that have to be met. First, your academic credentials must align with whatever piloting school you choose to go to.

There, you will also find that many things have to be fulfilled during the piloting course before you can be given the green light to fly aeroplanes. As a prospective pilot, you ought to prepare yourself financially, physically, and mentally before you make the bold step to join an aviation school.

How to become a pilot in Kenya

There are three ways to become an aircraft captain in Kenyan and worldwide. The first is by joining the military as a cadet officer and then training to become a military pilot. The military pays for everything, so you do not have to worry about fees.

The second way is by joining a training programme run by an airline, and in this, the fees are also catered for. Finally, the third and most common way of becoming a pilot is paying for flying lessons, usually in an aviation school.

Aviation courses and requirements

What are the requirements for aviation school? Minimum subject requirements to become a pilot include a mean grade of C in KCSE or an equivalent with at least grade C in English, Mathematics, Physics/Chemistry/Physical Science. In addition, most pilot schools in Kenya require KCSE mean grade of C+ or an equivalent with at least grade C+ in English, Mathematics, Geography, and any other science.

Any aspiring aircraft captain must score Grade A in Aeroplane Performance Rating, a test done by commercial airline pilots. Other basic requirements include:

  • Be at least 16 years of age or more.
  • A certificate indicating medical fitness issued by an accredited Aviation Medical examiner.
  • Copies of national identification card/passport/birth certificate and academic papers.
  • At least six passport size photos (3 normal size and 3 2cm x 2.5cm).
  • Pass three internal written evaluations in English, Mathematics, and Psychometric Testing.
  • At least five lessons of flight assessment
  • A certificate of good conduct.

Courses

There are several pilot courses one can pursue, including:

  • Private Pilot License (PPL): Offers basic and foundational flying skills
  • Commercial Pilot License (CPL): For flying commercial aircraft and is done after the PPL
  • Private Pilot License (Helicopters): Similar to PPL but for flying private helicopters
  • Commercial Pilot License (Helicopters): For flying commercial helicopters
  • Instrument Rating Training: Trains one how to fly using instruments
  • Multi-engine Rating: Trains one to fly multi-engine aircrafts
  • Airline Transport Pilot License (ATPL): Highest level and rigorous training for airline transport captains and pilots in command on big aircrafts

Fees for a piloting course

How much does aviation school cost in Kenya? One impediment for many people from becoming a pilot is the fees required. Given the demanding nature of piloting fees, how many years does it take to become a pilot? Training in Kenya is expensive and takes up to 18 months.

Alternatively, one can train outside the country, with the USA and South Africa being common destinations where it could take from six to 14 months and is cheaper compared to Kenya. However, the downside of studying outside is that once you come back into the country, you have to part with close to a million shillings as pilot license costs.

What makes a good pilot

Qualifications to become an aircraft captain are not only a matter of academics. There is a range of things one has to fulfil before being admitted into an aviation school, and some of those things might vary based on the demands of the institution. Some of the things looked into by aviation schools include:

Passion for flying

You cannot make a good a if you are not interested in flying. You must demonstrate that you are passionate about flying without any external influences or peer pressure before you can be accepted into an aviation school.

Aptitude

A prospective student must be taken up in the air for about five sessions to evaluate their potential and ability before being admitted to the school.

Discipline

Piloting requires a much-focused individual; hence one has to give up things such as smoking, miraa and all other forms of substance abuse. Short-cuts and the Kenyan matatu attitude is not condoned at aviation schools.

Education

Even though C+ is the minimum requirement, most aviation schools would like to see better KCSE results, preferably a B+ and above.

Health

There is no debate on good health in aviation. You must demonstrate good physical and mental health to be admitted into piloting. Be prepared for medical and drug tests before joining any aviation school in the country.

Pilot salary in Kenya

How much does a pilot earn in Kenya? It always depends on whether one is a private pilot or a commercial pilot. Private pilots earn based on their negotiation skills and how much their clients are willing to pay per hour of flight. On the other hand, commercial pilots are paid according to their rank and experience.

The pilot salary in Kenya is a very controversial subject, with expatriates earning higher than their Kenyan counterparts. An expatriate pilot takes home about Ksh. 716,802. Local Boeing pilots, on the other hand, earn Ksh. 483,000. On average, the lowest-earning pilots take home about Ksh. 132,000 while the highest-earning pocket a cool Ksh. 420,000 per month.

Steps to getting a pilot's license

  • A commercial pilot license can be obtained upon completing and submitting an application form.
  • The forms are available at the Kenya Civil Aviation Authority Branch offices or online at the KCAA website.
  • The filled form is submitted to the KCAA office or posting it to the Director-General, Kenya Civil Aviation Authority, P.O. Box 30163-00100, Nairobi, Kenya.

Documents that have to be attached with the application form include:

  • Aeronautical experience form indicating breakdown of the mandatory flight hours
  • Summarized edition of the flying experience form
  • An application form for English language proficiency endorsement
  • An application form for flight radiotelephony operator's license
  • At least four passport size photographs showing the full face
  • Class 1 valid medical certificate
  • Copies of I.D. or passport and certified copies of 2 of the last pages of the personal logbook
  • Pay the prescribed amount for license application fees and submit the payment receipt together with the application form.
