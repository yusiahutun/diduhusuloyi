Who is Seal?

The popular musician was born Seal Henry Olusegun Olumide Adeola Samuel on 19 February 1963 in Paddington, London, United Kingdom, to Francis Samuel (father) and Adebisi Ogundeji (mother). Francis was an Afro-Brazilian. He passed away in 1980 due to Kidney failure. Seal's parents divorced when he was barely five years old.

The British artist had a difficult upbringing. He was raised partly by his parents and partly by a foster family in Westminster, London. He has five siblings, although only his brother, Jeymes Samuel, and sister, Tanya, are known.

Jeymes (The Bullitts) is a singer-songwriter, music producer, and filmmaker. Tanya is a prominent businesswoman and owns a chain of boutiques in Lagos.

The artist went to Queens Park Community School. He also has a diploma in architecture. The Crazy singer was often teased at school due to extensive scars on his face. Seal was afflicted with a skin disease called lupus erythematosus at the age of 21, leaving extensive scars on his face.
