William Ruto Slams UDA MPs Unaware of 2,500 Jobs in Saudi Arabia: &quot;Do You Understand English?&quot;

 Posted on May 12, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  437 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • President William Ruto expressed disappointment at UDA Members of Parliament, claiming that they were not aware of nursing opportunities in Saudi Arabia
  • The interested 2,500 applicants were supposed to fly to Saudi Arabia in January 2024 but only 500 applications were received
  • The president demanded to know whether the lawmakers keep abreast of current and trending affairs through gazettes

Nancy Odindo, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics, news, and feature stories across digital and print media in Kenya.

President William Ruto has criticised members of the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) in parliament, alleging they were not informed about job opportunities for nurses in Saudi Arabia.

Why was William Ruto disappointed in UDA MPs?

During a joint National Executive Retreat and Parliamentary Group consultative meeting held in Naivasha, Nakuru, the president expressed his dismay that only 500 Kenyans had applied for the 2,500 announced nursing vacancies, with the intended departure to Saudi Arabia in January 2024.

The head of state voiced his disappointment and questioned whether the lawmakers were staying informed about current issues.

In a somewhat humorous tone, Ruto also raised concerns about the legislators' comprehension of English and wondered where they were tuning in when he addressed matters of national development.

"Let me ask, who was prohibited from sending 50 or 20? Are you saying you were not told? Where do you want to hear it from? Don't you read gazettes? And when I speak, do you think I talk nothing? Do you usually listen to me? Do you understand English?" he posed.

Ruto stated that the information was published in the media and that he has been constantly talking about it. He further stated that the remaining numbers need to be filled.

Government to export 2,500 skilled nurses to Saudi Arabia

The announcement came after the head of state visited Saudi Arabia in December 2023 to sign bilateral agreements with the Gulf state.

Through a gazette notice, the Ministry of Labour and Social Protection announced that 2,500 skilled nurses would be deployed in Saudi Arabia to work in the nursing and personal healthcare sectors.

"...we request for applications from qualified Kenyans for employment in the KSA as General Nurses and Personal Assistant or Healthcare Assistant professionals," read the notice signed by Labour PS Shadrack Mwadime in part.
