Nairobi PAG Church in Fear after &quot;Developers&quot; Issue Demolition Notice, Offer KSh 10k Compensation

 Posted on May 17, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  834 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon
  • Dr Aggrey Pentecostal Assemblies of God (PAG) church pastor was summoned by individuals who claimed they were implementers of a government slum upgrade project
  • The purported developers told the pastor that the church was in a public space and would be demolished, even offering KSh 10,000 as compensation
  • Nairobi county government has distanced itself from such a project and advised the church to follow the matter up with police to unravel the cartels behind the misinformation

Congregants of an Eastleigh-based church are a worried flock after individuals who identified themselves as "slum upgraders" issued them a verbal notice to vacate and demolish their sanctuary immediately.

Slum upgrading project implementers

The individuals who had purported to be working with the county and national government claimed that Dr Aggrey Pentecostal Assemblies of God (PAG) church was in a public space where a slum upgrading project was set to be implemented.

Through Pastor Isabella Khaduthi, the congregants who fellowship at the church disclosed that on Wednesday, December 14, 2022, they were summoned by the area administration.

The agenda of the meeting, she claimed was held at the area sub-county commissioner’s office, was to make them aware of the impending upgrade project and a verbal notice to vacate was granted too.

The church sits on plot No 12 at Lumbwa Road, Nairobi.

Summoning for vacation notice

“I was summoned by a man who said he was the area sub-county commissioner and identified himself as John Kipking’en. Together with other landlords in the area, we found the purported administrator with another man who said he was the assistant Chief," Khaduthi recalled.

In the office, the pastor said, they were introduced to some six people who identified themselves as “slum developers from the ministry of lands and housing”.

“They introduced themselves as slum developers and after speaking lengthily and without any written notice, they asked us to vacate to pave way for their project. They even offered to give us KSh 10,000 compensation for the impending demolition,” Khaduthi explained.

Khaduthi said they made a follow up in the ministry of lands and urbanisation and established that none of the six worked at the ministry.

"I remember one guy who introduced himself as John Muya working in the ministry of lands and urbanisation as a director."

It is for this reason that the church wrote to the county government of Nairobi, Ethics and Anticorruption Commission (EACC) and the Ministry of Lands asking them to intervene into the matter.

According to the church, they have legally occupied the land for over 30 years and are wondering how suddenly it has become public or someone’s land.

Letter to the Nairobi county government

In a letter seen by TUKO.co.ke, the Aggrey Church, through their lawyer Alice Gulenywa, raised the matter to Nairobi county government seeking intervention.

TUKO.co.ke’s John Papanoo however, confirmed from the county’s Planning Chief Officer John Analo that he was not aware of any such development project.

“It could have been forged because I see no such notice on my desk. No such letter has been copied to me,” Analo said.

The church’s lawyer said it was not adding up for his clients to get a letter of demolition from the county for a slum upgrade project the county itself seemed unaware of.

“The church has a valid lease and paid fees and land rates required by Nairobi city county in full. On January 3, 2023, you sent a group of people to pay KSh 10,000 to allow them continue with demolition,” the lawyer submitted to the county.

County unaware of slum upgrading project

In a response to a letter detailing the church’s complaints, Nairobi county through CEC for Environment and Urban Planning, Stephen Gathuita Mwangi , distanced itself from the allegations and advised the church to report the matter to police for in-depth investigations.

“Nairobi city county is not involved in the alleged slum upgrading and demolition, consequently we advise that your client reports the matter to the police for investigation and subsequent legal action,” the letter said.

Following this notice, structures next to the church have already gone down and occupants vacated with the church the only premises standing.

Members of the church appealed to President William Ruto and the civil society to intervene and save their sanctuary from the “faceless” people eyeing their premises.

Shortly after winning elections, Ruto's deputy Rigathi Gachagua disclosed that the Kenya Kwanza team had a memorandum of understanding with the church which he vowed would be fully adhered to by the current regime.
