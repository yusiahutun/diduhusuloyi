Which is the most expensive trophy in football? The top 10 list

 Posted on August 23, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1518 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Football is a loved sport worldwide. Throughout the year, numerous leagues run competitive matches, culminating in the winner being awarded a trophy. These trophies are valued differently, depending on many factors, including the materials used to make them. So, which is the most expensive trophy in football?

Which is the most expensive trophy in football? Winning a trophy is a dream for all players and teams. However, it is worth noting that some cups are valued higher than others. For instance, the FIFA World Cup trophy is valued higher than the English Premier League Cup.

The most expensive trophy in football

Soccer competitions, either at the club or national levels, are crowned with trophies to winners. As a result, most teams involved give their best to emerge winners. According to Egscholars, these are the top expensive trophies in football.

1. FIFA World Cup Trophy – $20 million

Valued at $20 million, the FIFA World Cup Trophy is the most expensive trophy in soccer. Since the advent of this tournament in 1930, only two trophies have been used. The Jules Rimet Trophy was used from 1930 to 1970, and the FIFA World Cup Trophy from 1974 to the present day.

FIFA World Cup Trophy was designed in 1974 by renowned Italian designer Silvio Gazzaniga. It depicts two athletes raising their arms in victory while holding the world in their hands. It is awarded to the winning team of the FIFA World Cup, held once every four years, and it is undoubtedly one of the most iconic trophies.

Since its inception, it has been the object of desire for every football-playing nation worldwide. It is 36.5 cm tall and is made of 6.175 kg of 18 karats (75%) gold. It has a 13 cm in-diameter circular base made of two layers of malachite. The winning team’s name is inscribed on the base of the trophy.

2. Copa Libertadores Trophy – $8.5 million

Copa Libertadores Trophy is the second most expensive trophy in the world. It is valued at a whopping $8.5 million. It is a trophy awarded annually by CONMEBOL to the football club that wins the Copa Libertadores.

Sterling silver dominates the top of the trophy. Bronze with a silver coating only makes up a modest portion of the crest’s design. Plywood from hardwood lumber is used to make the pedestal.

A club that wins the Copa Libertadores de América tournament for three consecutive seasons keeps the cup permanently. Additionally, the tournament winners keep the trophy until the next draw. Thereafter, they receive a replica and hand over the original.

3. UEFA Europa League Trophy – $4.5 million

The UEFA Europa League is an annual football club competition organised since 1971 by the Union of European Football Associations. It is the third most expensive trophy and is valued at $4.5 million.

UEFA Europa League Trophy is the second-tier competition in European club football. It sits between the UEFA Champions League and the UEFA Europa Conference League. It was created in 1972 by Bertoni.

The cup is predominantly made of silver, sitting on a yellow marble base. It is the heaviest as it weighs 15 kilograms. It is given to the champion of the European second-tier club competition each year.

4. The FA Cup Trophy – $1.18 million

The FA Cup is an annual knockout men's domestic English football competition. It is made from 6.3 kilograms of 925 sterling silver and stands 61.5 centimetres tall. Since its inception, it has undergone two major significant renovations.

The current FA Cup was commissioned in 2013 after about 250 hours of development. It was first awarded to Arsenal FC after their victory over Hull City in 2014. It is valued at around $1.18 million and remains one of the most recognisable trophies in football.

5. Ballon d’Or Trophy – $805,439

The Ballon d’Or Trophy has a pyrite base, measures 31 cm in height and 23 cm in diameter, and weighs just over 7 kg. It is football’s greatest individual honour, and many of the game’s elite players work hard to get it.

Ballon d'Or is French for the golden ball. It was first presented in 1956 by the French Football Magazine. It was made by a renowned French jewelling house called Mellerio dits Meller.

Cristiano Ronaldo sold one of his Ballon d'Or for £600,000 (about $805,439). Since then, this trophy's worth has been estimated at roughly that much.

6. African Cup of Nations Trophy – $150,000

African Cup of Nations is the main international men's association football competition in Africa. It is sanctioned by the Confederation of African Football (CAF) and was first held in 1957.

The cup was initially called the Abdelaziz Abdallah Salem Trophy. It was named in honour of the first CAF president. The team that wins the tournament three consecutive times keeps it forever.

The current iteration of the cup was designed in Italy and has a gold-plated finish. It is worth $150,000 and takes the number six spot on the list of expensive trophies in football.

7. Italian Serie A Trophy – $66,000

The Italian Serie A cup is a tall, funnel-shaped trophy. It is frequently referred to incorrectly as the "Scudetto", which means little shield. It is awarded to the winner of the top league in Italian football.

The cup was originally known as the Trophy of the Champions of Italy. It was designed by renowned sculptor Ettore Calvelli in 1960. It has a blue sodalite base and a gold ring in the middle depicting athletes.

The Italian Serie A trophy is 58 centimetres tall and 8 kilograms in weight. The names of all winning teams have their names inscribed on the current trophy. It is valued at $66,000.

8. Bundelisga Meisterschale – $57,102

The Bundesliga shield was designed and created by renowned art professor Elisabeth Treskow. It is given to the winners of the top-flight German football league. It has the names of all German league winners from 1903 engraved on it.

9. UEFA Champions League Trophy – $15,000

This is a trophy awarded annually by UEFA to the football club that wins the UEFA Champions League. Teams that win the league three consecutive times receive a multiple-winner badge.

The UEFA Champions League trophy has had a name, designs, and version modifications over the years. Jürg Stadelmann created its original form in 1968. It is 73.5 centimetres tall and weighs 7.5 kilograms.

10. The English Premier League Trophy – $10,000

The English Premier League is among the most loved tournaments worldwide. It attracts the interest of fans all over. Every year, 20 teams battle it out for the cup in home and away encounters.
