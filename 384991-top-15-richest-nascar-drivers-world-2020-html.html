Top 15 richest NASCAR drivers in the world as of 2020

 Posted on May 27, 2024  |  7 minutes  |  1314 words  |  Aldo Pusey

To successfully make a name in the sports industry, athletes have to put all the effort when participating in a competition and have a sharp brain that will help them invest. NASCAR drivers are some of the most affluent sportspeople in the industry. The richest NASCAR drivers in the world own teams and invest in thriving businesses outside of the driving.

NASCAR drivers also get a huge deal of endorsements. You will not be surprised to see your favourite driver during a super bowl commercial break or any other competition. For many, the first win usually marks the beginning of success, victory, and finishes.

Richest NASCAR drivers in the world

Who is the richest NASCAR driver? How much is she or he worth? The following are the top 15 richest NASCAR drivers in 2020:

1. Dale Earnhardt Jr – $400 million

Dale Earnhardt Jr is one of the highest-paid NASCAR drivers of all time. The star not only made a name and his wealth from his winnings but also merchandising. He has 25% of NASCAR’s merchandise sales and owns JR Motorsports. He has also invested in other sectors and runs successful businesses.

2. Jeff Gordon – $230 million

Jeff Gordon has won the Winston Cup champion four times. He is also the only man to win the Brickyard 400 four times, and a total of 98 victories. His wins have made him a millionaire in just a few years. He co-owns Jimmie Johnson’s racing team, scores of sponsorships, and his dealership. He also owns a wine company that adds up to his assets.

3. Jimmie Johnson – $150 million

Jimmie Johnson is almost feted as the only eight-time cup winner in NASCAR history. He has bagged 83 victories, 228 top-five finishes and 336 career top-ten finishes. A good amount of his assets also comes from merchandising and sponsorships.

4. Tony Stewart – $90 million

Tony Stewart has been the man to watch when NASCAR conversations take place. Some of his achievements include winning two brickyards, three-time Budweiser shootout winner, and two cup championships. The driver may have retired but he is still seen active in the scene. He is the co-owner of Stewart Haas racing with stakes in the cars of Clint Bowyer and Kevin Harwick.

5. Ken Schrader – $75 million

Ken Schrader has heavily invested in different businesses. He makes money from Federated Auto Parts Raceway which he fully owns and Macon Speedway which he is a part-owner. He is not as big in major races but he has made a name for himself in minor races.

6. Mark Martin – $75 million

Mark Martin has been retired for about 6 years now. The iconic NASCAR driver may not participate in any race but his legacy continues to shine on. He became the 1977 Rookie of the Year and had to battle with numerous losses before establishing himself as a pro. He owns a string of dealerships and Mark Martin Powersports in Alabama.

7. Kevin Harvick– $75 million

The super-competitive driver is one of only five men to win in Sprint and Xfinity Cup series. One of Harvick’s major source of wealth was his Kevin Harvick Incorporated team which he sold for a tidy sum. After selling his team, his attention drifted to racing. His other sources of income include endorsements.

8. Carl Edwards – $70 million

2005 is one of the greatest years for Carl Edwards. He won the Busch and Nextel series and was named the Rookie of the Year. No one could do better car backflips other than Carl Edwards at the time. Endorsement deals and other corporate features have also contributed to Edwards’ success.

9. Richard Petty – $70 million

No one could come close to Richard Petty during his prime. The guy knew his stuff and did all the races well. He is celebrated for most Daytona 500 victories, seven-time cup champion, most wins in a single season among other things. Even with the small prize winnings, Petty managed to make a fortune out of the cash he made.

10. Danica Patrick – $60 million

Danica Patrick was a media favourite. She was all over blogs and news stories for racing related news. She tried her best to compete with men though she only managed to bag a few wins. Danica used her fame to her advantage, making millions of dollars from magazines and super bowl ads.

11. Matt Kenseth – $60 million

When NASCAR drivers retire, many people forget about them for a minute and do not expect to see them in official races. It was a pleasant surprise when Matt Kenseth come from retirement in 2020 to join Chip Ganassi Racing. Kenseth replaced Kyle Larson who got himself into trouble. Public endorsements and brand-pushing are some of the things that made the driver rich.

12. Jeff Burton – $55 million

Jeff Burton is another great NASCAR driver who despite his skills never won a considerable championship. He, however, made enough money from the racing and endorsement deals before hanging up his boots. Today, he is a commentator in the same sport and seems to be grooming his son Harrison in NASCAR racing.

13. Greg Biffle - $55 million

Private affairs and flops have cost Greg Biffle a good chunk of money he could have made from his career. He is, however, still among the richest NASCAR drivers in the world. He won the Busch Series and was one of only three men to win Busch and Craftsman Truck Services. He joined NBC as an analyst and has been featured in several magazines and endorsements.

14. Denny Hamlin – $55 million

Denny Hamlin has 38 wins and is one of the annually highest-paid guys in NASCAR. He is also the fourth man to win back-to-back Daytona 500s in 2020. His face is popular on brands and you are sure to see him endorsing products that earn him millions.

15. Kyle Busch – $50 million

Kyle Busch is one of the wealthiest NASCAR drivers today. He is the first man in Monster Cup to win on every track he’s ever taken part in. His winnings could still make him an elite wealthy sports guy but his enthusiasm for the sport pushes him to do more. He owns Kyle Busch Motorsports, which is successful and continues to expand his wealth.

Marketing and product endorsements are among the major ways the richest NASCAR drivers in the world earn their income. A driver may fail to clutch the championship but participation may attract thousands of sponsors. Those starting have to catch up fast or less their names fail to appear among the greats. To these drivers, driving is not only a professional activity but also a lifestyle.
