  • The Nigerian has sparked reactions as he has been spotted accompanying his father-in-law on several occasions, contrary to African culture

President William Ruto's son-in-law Alexander Ezenagu has been trending for the better part of the week after he was spotted accompanying him to Uganda.

Ezenagu has also been pictured at State House, Nairobi, which is the official residence of the president of Kenya.

The Nigerian married Ruto's daughter, June, on Thursday, May 27, 2021, in a colourful ceremony at Karen.

Who is Alexander Ezenagu?

According to his LinkedIn profile, he's an assistant professor of taxation and commercial law at Hamad Bin Khalifa University (HBKU) in Qatar.

He's a barrister and solicitor of the Supreme Court of Nigeria.

Education at the University of Ibadan

He studied at the University of Ibadan from 2005 to 2010, graduating with a first-class degree in bachelor of law.

At the institution, he was the team leader of the Students In Free Enterprise (SIFE). He also doubled up as the president of the Literary and Debate Society.

Nigerian Law School

Ezenagu joined the Nigerian Law School in 2010 and scored an impressive first-class grade in his bar certificate.

University of Cambridge

Between 2013 and 2014, he studied at the prestigious University of Cambridge in England.

He obtained his master of law from the institution.

McGill University

In 2015, Ezenagu enrolled at McGill University to pursue a doctor of philosophy in international tax law.

He obtained his PhD in international tax law from the Canadian University in 2019.

Ezenagu's working experience

The learned man has worked in several companies. He began his career as an associate at Templars, Lagos, in 2014, before working briefly at Ernst & Young.

In February 2017, he was employed as an international tax researcher by the International Centre for Tax and Development (ICTD) in the United Kingdom.

From 2015 to date, he has served as an associate research fellow for Nigeria's Oil, Gas, Energy, Environment and Sustainable Development.

He joined Hamad Bin Khalifa University in January 2020 as a full-time assistant professor of taxation and commercial law.

Besides his role at HBKU, he's a consultant to the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD), the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), the Coalition for Dialogue on Africa (CODA), TaxCOOP Canada, Tax Justice Network Africa (TJNA) and other international organisations.

Martha Koome's daughter working for Rockefeller Foundation

In other news, TUKO.co.ke reported about Martha Koome's daughter, Kagwiria Koome, who's working as the manager, food initiative at the Rockefeller Foundation.

Koome was appointed chief justice in May 2021 following a successful interview by the Judicial Service Commission (JSC).

Kagwiria has worked at the multi-billion organisation for eight years.

Forbes reported that the foundation was founded in 1858 by the Rockefeller family, ranked 43rd on the list of America's richest families in 2020, with a net worth of $8.4 billion (KSh 1.015 trillion).

Since its inception, the foundation has distributed over $22 billion (KSh 2.7 trillion) to charity, according to its website.
