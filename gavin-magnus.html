What is Gavin Magnus’ net worth? Who is Gavin Magnus’ girlfriend?

Introduction

Gavin Magnus may still be a minor—he turned 16 in 2023—but he’s a major celebrity with a hardcore tween following.

The California boy is ultra-popular on Instagram and YouTube, and he’s put in some on-screen hours with Nickelodeon and Cartoon Network, too.

The young DreamworksTV host has an impressive discography to his name, and his official music videos have garnered tens of millions of combined views.

His cover of Shawn Mendes’ Senorita—featuring his on-again, off-again girlfriend Coco Quinn—had gathered 56 million views by 2021.

So, where did this seemingly overnight sensation come from? And where is he going next? Here’s what we know.

The Origin Story

Gavin Magnus was born on the 26th of March 2007, in Placentia, California.

Gavin is the youngest child of Theresa and Dave Magnus and the little brother of Jakob and Justin.

All three brothers live with their parents in Placentia, where the boys attended school until they started home-schooling when Gavin was in the fifth grade.

Magnus dated social media darling Piper Rockelle in 2019, which helped drive his rising star even faster—but the pair broke up shortly after.

In 2020 and 2021, he was in an on / off relationship with Coco Quinn.

@Getty Images

In a video posted to Gavin’s channel in March 2021, his mom rates his girlfriends—Piper, Coco, and his first-ever girlfriend, Reece.

The TV Star

Gavin has always enjoyed performing, according to his parents, and liked to put on his own shows as a child.

He has starred in several small roles on Nickelodeon and the Cartoon Network, including a part on the 2012 Dreamworks Spooky Stories—when he was just 5 years old.

His other on-screen roles have included Charlie in the 2017 TV doco, Unusual Suspects, Byron in Timecrafters: The Treasure of Pirate’s Cove, and Parker Bennett in Reckless Juliets.

The Music Man

@Getty Images

Although Gavin has always said he loves both acting and singing, in a 2020 interview, he admitted that singing was his true love.

And it’s easy to believe when you look at his discography so far.

From his early days with the little-known track, Hollaback, in 2018, fans were drawing comparisons between Magnus and a young Justin Bieber.

Then came tracks like Crushin’ in 2019, A Whole ‘Nother Level, Catching Feelings, and Hearts on a Pendant in 2020, and 2021’s Fake Love.

On top of his own singles and later an EP, he also recorded Pop It for the Next Level motion picture soundtrack.

The Dark Side of Fame

Gavin Magnus may have been flattered by the early comparisons to one of his idols, Justin Bieber.

But along with the confidence boost of being compared to one of the most successful singers of a generation, there was a dark side to the fame game: bullying.

The TikTok and small screen star shared that he had been bullied severely in the 5th and 6th grade when he spent some time in a local public school.

The tween had enjoyed some early success with his music, and his most recent music video—Crushin’—was nearing 10 million views on YouTube.

This drew the wrong kind of attention at a California public school. “Kids would call me Justin Bieber, and I would get made fun of. I was bullied a lot.”

Talking specifics, the blond-haired, blue-eyed youngster shared, “Kids would grab my backpack and throw me on the ground.”

Fortunately, the early brush with the downside of fame didn’t break the young entertainer. He joined a social media anti-bullying campaign and works to make sure other kids have his support as they go through similar situations.

“Someone really cares about them,” he explains. “I just want everyone to know that they’re not alone.”

Influences

@Getty Images

Being called Justin Bieber may have been negative in middle school, but that didn’t deter Gavin—he had long looked up to the high-profile singer and heartthrob as an idol.

He admitted in a 2019 interview that he used to dress up like Biebs and do shows for his family and friends.

He also considered Juice WRLD one of his biggest inspirations, explaining that his music got him through a lot of difficult situations. “I love him a lot,” said the young Magnus.

So what does the future hold for a young California kid with big ambitions and a love of performing?

He wants to do a lot more touring. He also shares that he never gets stage fright and loves performing. “I never get nervous—I could have the whole world watching, and I would not care.”
