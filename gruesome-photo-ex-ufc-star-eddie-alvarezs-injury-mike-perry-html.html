Former UFC star Eddie Alvarez suffered a horrific eye injury during his clash with Mike Perry on Saturday night. 

The pair headlined a highly-anticipated Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship card in Salt Lake City over the weekend.

The opening round saw both fighters attack aggressively, with the pair landing brutal combinations. Former UFC welterweight contender Perry took control of the second round, landing several power strikes to Alvarez's head.


Alvarez ended the round strongly, however, following the second round, his corner stopped the fight due to severe damage he suffered to his left eye. 

After the bout, the former UFC lightweight champion was left with one eye completely shut.

Fans were able to clearly see the damage Alvarez suffered during the post-fight press conference.

Speaking after the clash with Perry, Alvarez said: 'I think bare-knuckle is the fastest-growing sport in the world. I love this, I really do. I was on point.

'I was so sharp. Unfortunately this sport is so volatile, you just don't know. I think my orbital's smashed up and that's all she wrote.

'It f*****g sucks, it was a good show, the fans won; I didn't win, but the fans won. 

'I thought Mike would go down and he didn't, my hat goes off to Mike Perry, the guy's a f*****g battle axe.

'Mike was the better man, I take my wins and I take my losses the same way and keep my head high.

'I'm not 100 per cent sure when he landed the shot he landed, but midway though the second I felt my eye closing and when I sat down I went to open my mouth a bit and I think my orbital is smashed up a bit.'

The defeat was Alvarez's first since signing with BKFC and is currently 1-1 following his debut victory against former UFC star Chad Mendes.

The 39-year-old enjoyed a successful stint with the UFC - and even won the lightweight title in 2016

The 39-year-old enjoyed a successful spell with the UFC and even struck gold when he defeated Rafael dos Anjos for the lightweight strap. 

In the first defence of his title, he was beaten by Conor McGregor at UFC 205 back in 2016.

During his stint with MMA's biggest promotion, he defeated the likes of Justin Gaethje, Anthony Pettis and Gilbert Melendez - before leaving for ONE Championship in 2019.
