Meghan Markle's mistake of 'wanting to have her way' was 'delusional' - expert

 Posted on June 13, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  688 words  |  Martina Birk

A royal expert has chimed in on the Duke and Duchess of Sussex. They’ve branded Meghan Markle’s thinking as ‘delusional’ for ‘underestimating’ The Firm.

Meghan Markle has been criticized by a relationship expert for “underestimating” The Firm when she got married for the second time. The expert has claimed the royal would have understood the scrutiny she faced after tying the knot with the Duke of Sussex. Therefore, the expert slammed this way of thinking from Meghan Markle as ‘delusional’. Following Prince Harry and Meghan’s sudden exit as working royals, they carved out a new life in Los Angeles. Since then, fans and social media across the world have been watching their every move.

Meghan Markle’s wish branded ‘simply delusional’

Relationship expert Kate Mansfield has weighed on the aftermath of the Sussexes’ stepping down in 2020.

She didn’t hold back when she explained the former TV star “completely underestimated the reality” of what it means to marry a royal. She went as far as claiming Meghan Markle’s wish of privacy was ‘delusional’.

Mansfield told GB News: “I think that one of the reasons that Meghan has had so much negative press is that she seems to have completely underestimated the reality of what it means to be married into the Royal Family.

“Wanting privacy and to have her way about things is simply delusional.

“For the common person looking in, although we might have some sympathy for the invasion of privacy, the fact that she married into such huge privilege counters this.”

However, in terms of advice for the Suits actress, Mansfield only had one thing to say.

“Meghan will likely have to suck it up and bear it,” were the words of wisdom from the expert.

Meghan and Harry embraced by the celebrity world

It comes after a friend of Meghan’s dad, Thomas Markle, almost shattered their dreams of a quiet life in California.

A royal and celebrity sightseeing tour promised to transport passengers right in front of the duo’s Montecito mansion. Fans were expected to splash out $1,200 for the price for six people. This would have been in a bid to get a glance of the Duke and Duchess of Sussex’s home. Aside from the mansion, they’d see their favorite hotspots in the Hollywood town.

The trips were first advertised as the Royal Celebrity Tour of Los Angeles and Montecito. They were also going to be run by a pal of Meghan’s estranged father, Karl Larsen

In a trip down memory lane, fans were going to be taken to see nostalgic areas. For instance, the polo club where the Duchess – mom to Archie and Lilibet – kissed Harry last year. There were also set visits to her favorite shops and restaurants.

The trip has since been scrapped after only a few days of it being online.

Tour’s fiery backlash

Karl Larsen initially listed the tour on Tripening with the description: “I’m the world-famous celebrity photographer Karl Larsen, a best friend of Thomas Markle (Meghan’s father) and the guy with a stunning exclusive portfolio of pictures of Prince Harry and Meghan Markle together in Montecito.”

However, tech entrepreneur Christopher Bouzy spoke out and criticized the tour. He told Newsweek it was an act of “targeted harassment” at the royal pair before it was taken down.

Bouzy had slammed the tour after the news first came out. He said: “The problematic listing was an act of targeted harassment, and it jeopardized the safety of the Duke and Duchess’s family.

“The rise in online harassment underscores the urgent need for robust legislation to counteract this escalating problem that affects everyone.”

The tour was axed by the travel firm it was advertised on following concerns over privacy.

Larsen then clarified to the outlet that he didn’t plan to drive past Harry and Meghan’s mansion.

Despite the backlash, and the tour being removed, Larsen said it is “not going to be the end.” Designed for true fans, he compared it to Elvis Presley fans visiting Graceland. He explained he’d like the tour to focus on the areas where they’ve been seen and where she grew up.
