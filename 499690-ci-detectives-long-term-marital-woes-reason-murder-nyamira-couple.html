Nyamira Family Claims Fight Over Ruai Land Could be Reason Behind Brutal Murder of Couple

 Posted on July 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  405 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • The bodies of 62-year-old Edward Morema Nyagechi and Mong’ina Morema, 60, were discovered in their house on Tuesday, March 21
  • According to family members, a piece of land they recently acquired in Ruai could be the reason behind their deaths
  • Details indicate that the parcel had had been a topic of discussion for a while, only for the two to end up death in their house in Nyamira

News of the brutal murder of a US-based Kenyan man and his wife in Nyamira County hit Kenyans like a shock wave a few days ago.

This was after the bodies of 62-year-old Edward Morema Nyagechi and Mong’ina Morema, 60, were discovered in their house on Tuesday, March 21.

Evidence indicated that the couple was brutally murdered by individuals who are yet to be identified.

As investigations into the homicide continue, officers from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations have indicated that the deaths could be related to marital woes.

Deaths could be over Ruai land

According to information from one of the family members, the deceased professor and his wife had recently acquired a parcel of land in Ruai.

The relative who spoke anonymously indicated that they suspect the land could be the reason behind the deaths because it had been a topic of discussion for a while.

Masaba North Sub-County Police boss Robert Ndambiri told the media that detectives were trying to establish how the attackers got into their house because there were no signs of forced entry.

“We have arrested two people as we commence our investigations. They are a housemaid who used to live with the deceased in their single-storey house and a farm hand,” he said, as reported by Daily Nation.

Couple was hit on heads by blunt object

Wounds on the bodies of the two victims shows that they were not only hit with a blunt object on their heads, but there were also stab wounds on other parts.

The two were hailed as very good and helpful people who helped their people even when living abroad.

Edward was the County Coordinator and Manager of a community group known as Bwakire Se Movement.
