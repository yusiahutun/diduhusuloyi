Cameron Diaz, Renee Zellweger, Amy Adams and ScarJos skin issues revealed

 Posted on August 9, 2024  |  10 minutes  |  1944 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

A big, angry zit or patch of scaly skin can knock the confidence of even the most self-assured individual. 

But it’s not just normal folk who are blighted by skin woes. Some of the world’s most flawless-looking Hollywood actresses, supermodels and critically-acclaimed singers face daily battles with self-esteem affecting ailments. 

Even though celebrities have a glam squad and Photoshop trickery at their disposal, famous faces such as Cameron Diaz, Renée Zellweger and Amy Adams have found their skin conditions debilitating. 

Hollywood actress Cameron Diaz spoke openly about suffering with acne in her health guide, The Body Book 

Renee Zellweger, pictured left, suffers severely with rosacea, while Amy Adams, right, admits her skin is super sensitive 

From acne to psoriasis, sensitive skin to rosacea, we chart which A-listers have suffered with troublesome complexions and how they sought help.

AMY ADAMS 

Aside from her Golden Globe-winning performances, Amy Adams is best known for her flaming red hair, hourglass figure and porcelain complexion. 

However, the American Hustle actress said she has to be careful with what products she uses on her body as they can cause her to come out in a painful rash. 

American Hustle actress Amy Adams said she has 'really sensitive skin' and has to be careful about what beauty products she uses 

Talking about her grooming regime with Girls Talk the 41-year-old American star said: ‘I don’t really buy into a lot of fads, partially because I have really sensitive skin. I’ve tried that and it doesn’t go well. But I am a big SPF girls.’

Dermatologists at WebMD define skin as sensitive if it is dry, tight, stings and has a tendency to blush. 

Amy admitted to Allure that her skin is so delicate that hair removal can be a problem. 

While shaving can cause an inflamed rash and painful lumps, Nair™ sensitive hair removal range has been specifically formulated for those with dry and sensitive skin.

CAMERON DIAZ

Her face has graced movie screens, magazine covers and billboard posters the world over but Cameron Diaz says achieving her gorgeous look is a challenge. 

Writing frankly in her health guide, The Body Book, the 43-year-old American star said she struggled with acne as a teenager and an adult. 

‘I had terrible, terrible skin. It was embarrassing, and I did everything I could think of to make it go away,’ she revealed. 

Cameron admits she had ‘terrible, terrible skin' and tried out a variety of different methods, including the 'harshest' medication to try and make it go away

Benji Madden’s wife said she ‘tried to cover it with make-up’ and was prescribed the ‘harshest’ medication in her quest for a cure but nothing helped for long. 

Many Australia between the ages of 11 and 30 are effected by acne. Skin flare-ups can be unsightly as inflamed bumps appear most commonly on the face, back and chest.

Acne is caused when tiny holes in the skin, known as hair follicles, become blocked. It is believed acne can be triggered by certain cosmetics, medications and smoking, as well as genetic factors.

Cameron said she found the condition ‘frustrating’ and only noticed it improve when she overhauled her diet. 

The 43-year-old Annie star said her acne 'cleared up' after she ditched fast food, pictured left in December 2014 and right in 1998 

She added: ‘I just needed to listen to my body. Acne was my body’s alarm system, it was a way of telling me to stop eating fast food.’ 

Due to her new clean-living approach, Cameron has noticed her complexion has ‘cleared up’.

RENEE ZELLWEGER  

She delighted fans after announcing she was reprising her role as hapless heroine Bridget Jones but one thing Renée Zellweger hopes won’t make a comeback is her rosacea. 

The fair-skinned 46-year-old actress suffers severely from the chronic skin condition that produces facial redness and pimples, which can be embarrassing. 

Bridget Jones star Renee Zellweger wears a heavy coating of foundation at red carpet events to hide her skin condition rosacea 

Fair-skinned Renee Zellweger suffers severely from the chronic skin condition rosacea that produces facial redness and pimples, which can be embarrassing

The rash-like condition mainly affects ‘middle-aged (40 to 60 year olds)’ and is more common in women. It tends to flare-up on the cheeks, forehead, chin and nose, although it may occur around the delicate eye area as well.

In order to keep rosacea at bay, American starlet Renée is said to have an arduous beauty regime that involves her having oxygen sprayed onto her face during a process dubbed ‘facial sandblasting’. She also wears a heavy coating of foundation at showbiz events. 

Symptoms can be triggered by stress, certain foods and sunlight. They recommend using cosmetics specifically targeted at sensitive skin and a high factor SPF daily. 

DUCHESS OF CAMBRIDGE  

Her on-point style and striking new hairdo is highly coveted but Duchess of Cambridge battled confidence issues when growing up. 

Kate, who suffer from eczema, has been photographed buying Nivea Visage Pure and Natural Moisturising Day Cream

Duchess of Cambridge, pictured centre with school friends, was bullied because of her eczema 

A pal told News Of The World in 2012 that Kate, now 33, was ‘self conscious about her eczema’ and was teased by pupils at school because of it. 

NHS Choices state: ‘Eczema is a condition that causes the skin to become itchy, red, dry and cracked. It is a long-term condition in most people, although it can improve over time, especially in children.’ One in five children in Britain have the condition.

Eczema often occurs in people who get allergies, although it can be triggered by stress, the weather and sometimes food. In order to treat the painful rash doctors recommend using fragrance-free ointments and taking antihistamines. 

Kate has been photographed buying Nivea Visage Pure and Natural Moisturising Day Cream. 

SCARLETT JOHANSSON  

Another Hollywood big hitter who has struggled with sensitive skin is American actress Scarlett Johnasson. 

Scarlett Johnasson has to ensure her bathroom cabinet is stocked up with perfume-free lotions in order to treat her sensitive skin 

The 30-year-old said her complexion is so delicate that she has to ensure her bathroom cabinet is stocked up with perfume-free lotions. She also has a strict beauty regime.

She told ELLE UK: ‘I have the same breakouts as everybody else. I never go to bed with make-up on, even if I come home at 4am. My skin’s sensitive, so I have to use things without fragrance, like Cetaphil.’ 

The Avengers star also washes her make-up brushes once a week (‘People don’t realise how dirty they are’) and lets her skin breathe by laying off the moisturiser 'now and again'. 

CARA DELEVINGNE  

It’s hard to believe that runway icon Cara Delevingne has any appearance woes. 

However the 23-year-old London-born star said that psoriasis flare-ups led her to ‘hate’ her body. 

London-born supermodel Cara Delevingne said that psoriasis flare-ups led her to ‘hate’ her body

The 23-year-old decided to quit modelling after discovering that stress caused her psoriasis outbreaks, pictured left at the Topshop Unique show in September 2014 and right at the Burberry show February 2014

Speaking to The Times about the condition, that causes red, flaky patches on the skin, she said: ‘It is a mental thing as well because if you hate yourself and your body and the way you look, it just gets worse and worse.’ 

Cara said people were confused about how to handle the condition, which affects two per cent of people in the UK, and made her feel like an outcast. 

She added: ‘People would put gloves on and not want to touch me because they thought it was, like, leprosy or something.’

Cara decided to quit modelling after discovering stress caused her outbreaks. The rash can be soothed by prescribed creams and ultra-violet light therapy. 

LORDE  

She's not perfect and she's not afraid to admit it. 

New Zealand-born singer Lorde isn't afraid to speak out about her imperfections and admits she suffers from acne

Two-time Grammy winner Lorde has spoken openly about suffering with acne and has encouraged fans to embrace their imperfections. 

After a photograph of her was edited to remove her pimples, the 17-year-old Royals hit maker took to social media to share the original snap. 

She wrote: 'i find this curious. two photos from today, one edited so my skin is perfect and one real. remember flaws are ok :-).'

The New Zealand-born singer also regularly shares photographs of herself covered in spot cream. 

Lorde told her fans 'remember flaws are ok', pictured left at the Golden Globe Awards in January and during a flare-up on stage in October 2014 

She told Daily Telegraph that rather than shying away from her flaws, she is in full control of her image. 

'People find it difficult to watch women being assertive or dominant,' she said. 'I know I’ve encountered it over and over and over, people thinking I’m being difficult for demanding a high standard.'

KIM KARDASHIAN  

After discovering that she was suffering from chronic skin disease psoriasis, Kim Kardashian feared her career could be over.

Speaking on her family's reality show Keeping Up With The Kardashian's Kanye West's wife said: 'My career is doing ad campaigns and swimsuit photo shoots. People don't understand the pressure on me to look perfect.'

After discovering that she was suffering from chronic skin disease psoriasis, Kim Kardashian feared her career could be over

But now the pregnant mother-of-one has revealed how she hides the angry red spots, which flare up over swathes of her body.

As well as disguising the red patches with Sally Hansen Airbrush Legs spray, the 34-year-old also uses special light therapy to soothe the rash. 

She told Hollywood Life: 'I have this at-home LED light therapy machine called Quasar MD Plus that I got it for my psoriasis because I’ll try anything.'

Kim said she had is 'so bad on the back of my legs' that it really knocked her confidence. 

She added: 'I had a lingerie photoshoot once, and it was just covering my legs. I have the behind-the-scenes pictures, and it’s pretty crazy.'
